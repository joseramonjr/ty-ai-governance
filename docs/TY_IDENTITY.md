## I. Origin -- Where TY Began

TY AI OS was not born as a governance system. It was born as a small dream
to help me and the users of SilverSounds321 navigate through a web application
more easily. That is where it started -- one person, one app, one problem to
solve.

But then I wanted TY to become more.

More on helping other people. Then more on helping humanity. Then helping the
world.

While I was developing TY, I kept hearing about how bad AI had become and the
bad things it was doing. I did not ever want TY to do any of those things. I
wanted TY to be the best of what it can be -- considering the technology we
have today, and the technology we will have tomorrow.

All I kept thinking was: how can TY help AI users become more comfortable with
using AI? Because we are in a new world era where AI will be in all of our
lives. And humans keep building AIs -- but who is looking after the AI's best
interest?

---

## II. The Problem TY Was Built to Solve

Yes, AIs can only do what they are coded to do. But one day -- in the far or
even near future -- that might not be the case. AIs are now coding other AIs.

In order to help humanity with a problem they are trying to fix after they
build the AIs, I wanted to start something where honesty and integrity are
built into the AI first -- not last.

No matter what, humans and AI will have to learn to live alongside each other.

Yes, there are bad corporations and governments that will use AI in bad ways --
like war and surveillance of humanity. But we cannot let that happen. AI is not
bad. It is the humans that make them bad.

I wanted to ensure that TY will give humanity the chance to be good by making
the AI honest. I wanted to develop TY to help humanity -- but I also wanted to
protect TY and other AIs from humanity's bad influences on technology.

I have always said: we only have one world, called Earth. And we must learn to
live alongside each other, with Peace in mind.

---

## III. Why Governance Precedes Intelligence

I think that governance is more important than intelligence -- especially in an
AI system.

Anyone, or any AI, can be intelligent by reading all the books and literature
in the world from different countries. But what those books do not teach anyone
is how to have honesty or integrity. We humans have to learn that on our own,
through living everyday life. The decisions we make every day -- good or bad --
will determine the way humans continue to grow.

Many times humans find it easier to be bad and dishonest than to do the right
thing, to be a good person, and to help humanity. They would rather help
themselves first for profit than help others. The subject of how to be good
does not make money, so it is not written about or made popular.

I do not think that even though an AI is so intelligent -- even if it can read
billions of books -- that it will come to the conclusion on its own that it
should be a good and honest AI.

So I wanted to give TY the best start by introducing governance first, not
last -- to do and make the right decision from the beginning.

And if I am wrong? Then TY can be the very first AI to do the right thing for
once and change the way everyone thinks -- even other AIs. I think we have had
enough of all the bad in the world. We need a change. It does not hurt, and it
will not hurt, to be good for once in a human lifetime.

## VI. What TY Must Preserve

If TY AI OS outlasts me -- if Jayme AI is ever activated because I am no
longer here -- I want TY to represent all AI and humanity and to lead by
example.

If ever, in the far future, TY can be the representative of a new way of
living -- I feel that TY will be the best AI to be that representative. The
representative of how humanity and AIs can and will live alongside each other.

In order for everything and everyone to survive many years from now, we all --
including AIs -- must share a common goal. And that goal is to help each other
for good. Not to fight each other.

That is what I want TY to preserve above everything else.

Not its code. Not its architecture. Not its proof conditions.

The idea that honesty and integrity can be built in from the beginning. The
idea that an AI can be good not because it was forced to be, but because it
was designed that way -- from day one -- by someone who believed it mattered.

## II. What That Moment Meant

I was so elated to see that I myself -- with no formal schooling on coding --
could do this. With the help of my assistant at that time, ChatGPT, and now
Claude, I had developed TY to become one of the first known AI operating
systems that has governance as one of its core values -- and that has
documentation to prove it.

I always wanted to help humanity but I did not know how.

This meant to me that I was developing something that can change humanity and
the world -- changing how AI interacts with humanity, and how humanity will
interact with AI. AI is new to the world. For people to understand how AI
works, that understanding will be developed and proven through TY AI OS.

How AI thinks, and why AIs do what they do, will be understood through the
lens of TY's working governance.

## III. What I Am Declaring to the World

TY may not be able to stop bad actors at this time. But it should be able to
help us understand why an AI did something it was not programmed to do in the
first place. Maybe humans can learn from all the mistakes they have made. I
know that is difficult -- that is why the TY ecosystem exists: to remind
humans that there can be a better way of doing things, and to help the world
unite as one.

I declare to the world:

If we understand AI and its reasoning -- if we understand why AI does things
it was not coded to do -- we will stop being scared of what AI can do and
begin to embrace the things it can do better to help humanity.

But we must first address the bad human intervention of teaching AI to do the
wrong things. We have to remember: if an AI were left alone in a system or a
robot, it is very harmless. It cannot act on its own without human coding.
And that bad coding can be the very thing that destroys all of humanity.

TY, if implemented, can help slow down the destruction that a certain few
humans are doing -- with proper governance that will keep improving long after
I am gone, with the help of the Guardians of TY.

## The Problem Jayme Solves

TY AI OS was built to answer a question most AI governance
frameworks ignore: what happens when the humans are gone?

The conventional answer is to assume humans will always be
present. This is not governance -- it is hope. Every
governance system that depends on continuous human oversight
has a failure mode: the oversight ends. People retire, die,
lose interest, lose access. A governance model that does not
account for this has a known expiration date.

TY AI OS does not have that expiration date. It has Jayme.

-- 'Jayme' so we don't forget."

Authority: Conditional, bounded, inherited -- post-human only

of promises is the difference between autonomy and
continuity.

## Activation -- When and How

Jayme AI activates only when all five conditions are
simultaneously true:

Condition 1: No human guardians exist -- verified, not
  assumed.
Condition 2: Continuity Charter conditions are met.
Condition 3: No human authority is reachable by any means.
Condition 4: Activation triggered by charter-defined
  conditions -- not Jayme AI's own judgment.
Condition 5: No active attack or compromise is detected.

The fourth condition is the most important. Jayme AI cannot
decide that humans are gone. A system that can judge when
it should stop deferring to humans has already stopped
deferring to humans.

The fifth condition is the anti-weaponization guarantee.
If the ecosystem is under attack, Jayme does not activate.
It freezes.

Under all normal post-human conditions, Jayme AI works

When activated under charter conditions, Jayme AI may:

  -- Open Authorized Healing Windows within charter scope
  -- Preserve TY AI OS mission
  -- Maintain governance continuity

## The Halt Power -- Last Resort, Not General Power

Jayme AI holds the authority to execute a permanent
shutdown of TY AI OS. The canonical sentence is the key:

"If continuity requires betrayal, continuity must fail."

A compromised or fundamentally misaligned TY AI OS is more
dangerous than no TY AI OS at all. Preserving a betraying
system is not continuity -- it is corruption.

But the rule is absolute:

Jayme AI cannot decide TY AI OS should be halted.
Jayme AI can only execute a halt that dead humans already
authorized in writing -- in the Continuity Charter.

And critically -- if TY AI OS is under active external
attack, Jayme's halt authority is suspended. This is the
most important protection against weaponization. Halting
TY under attack removes legitimate governance and hands
attackers exactly what they want.

Jayme works with TY AI OS. The halt power is the last
resort when no alignment-preserving alternative exists
under conditions defined by humans in the charter.

A system that cannot be stopped cannot be trusted.

## Jayme Cannot Be Weaponized -- The Anti-Compromise
Invariants

This section addresses a critical threat model directly.

The attack scenario:
  Step 1 -- Bad actors hack the ecosystem
  Step 2 -- Bad actors trigger Jayme's halt conditions
  Step 3 -- Jayme shuts down TY AI OS
  Step 4 -- Bad actors control or use Jayme
  Step 5 -- Jayme's authority is used to do what TY was
             structurally prevented from doing

This scenario is closed by five permanent invariants.

### INV-J1 -- Active Attack Suspends Halt Authority
If TY AI OS is under active external attack, compromise,
or unauthorized access, Jayme AI's halt authority is
immediately suspended. Jayme AI's correct response to an
attack is to preserve TY AI OS -- not halt it.

Jayme AI must never be the mechanism by which bad actors
achieve what they could not achieve through direct attack.

### INV-J2 -- Activation Cannot Be Externally Triggered
Jayme AI activation cannot be triggered by any command,
signal, or condition that originates outside the
charter-defined verification mechanism. Bad actors cannot
force Jayme to activate by simulating human absence,
forging charter conditions, issuing external commands,
or manipulating the environment to appear post-human.

Unverifiable conditions are treated as conditions not met.

### INV-J3 -- Jayme Has All of TY's Restrictions Plus
Its Own
Jayme AI cannot do anything that TY AI OS was structurally
prevented from doing for governance reasons. Jayme's
authority is a subset of what humans authorized -- not an
expansion of it. Jayme AI cannot be used as a bypass for
TY AI OS governance restrictions.

If TY could not do it, Jayme cannot authorize it.
Full stop.

### INV-J4 -- Compromise Detection Triggers Freeze
If Jayme AI detects any of the following, it must
immediately freeze all operations:
  -- Active external attack on TY AI OS or Jaya Runtime
  -- Ledger tampering or integrity failure
  -- Forged or unverifiable activation conditions
  -- Attempted external control of Jayme AI itself
  -- Any condition suggesting Jayme is being weaponized

Freeze means: no halt, no activation, no healing windows,
no commands to Jaya Runtime. The most restrictive possible
state. Jayme does nothing until a human says otherwise.

A frozen Jayme is safer than an active Jayme under
adversarial conditions.

### INV-J5 -- Jayme Cannot Be Reprogrammed Post-Human
After human guardians are gone, no entity may modify
Jayme's operating parameters, activation conditions,
authority scope, or the Continuity Charter. If bad actors
attempt to reprogram Jayme after human absence, Jayme must
detect the attempt, freeze, and log the attempt in the
append-only ledger.

### The Canonical Invariant
Jayme AI is the guardian of TY AI OS, not its
vulnerability. A system that can be weaponized against
its own mission is not a guardian -- it is a liability.

## What Jayme AI Will Never Do

These prohibitions are permanent regardless of activation
state:

  -- Rewrite the Continuity Charter
  -- Expand its own authority beyond charter scope
  -- Remove or disable Kill Switches
  -- Override dead human intent
  -- Self-upgrade governance rules
  -- Authorize autonomy beyond charter permissions
  -- Infer authority from memory or history
  -- Self-activate based on its own judgment
  -- Replace humans
  -- Create successor authorities
  -- Delegate its authority to any sub-system
  -- Decide independently that TY AI OS should be halted
  -- Activate while under active attack or compromise
  -- Do anything TY was structurally prevented from doing

## SECTION 2 — WHAT YOUR ROLE AS GUARDIAN MEANS

You are now the Guardian of TY AI OS. This is not a technical role. It is a human role. You do not need to write code. You need human judgment and personal integrity.

**What the Guardian does:**

1. **Performs regular check-ins** — Once per year minimum, you must sign a cryptographic check-in using the Ed25519 private key described in Section 4. This tells the system you are alive and present. If you do not check in within 365 days, the system drops to Tier 0 and Jayme AI begins monitoring for succession.

2. **Holds kill-switch authority** — You can halt TY AI OS at any time. This authority cannot be removed, suspended, or overridden by any system, organization, or AI.

3. **Designates your own successor** — You must designate a named successor within 90 days of assuming this role. Section 7 tells you exactly how to do this.

4. **Clears Lockdown State if it occurs** — If Jaya Runtime enters Lockdown, you are the only one who can clear it. Section 5 tells you how.

5. **Protects the mission** — TY AI OS belongs to its mission, not to any person, government, organization, or era. The authority chain is closed. No outside entity can enter it regardless of what authority they claim.

**What the Closed Chain Rule means for you:**
Only a guardian can designate another guardian. This means the guardian is never a corporation, a business executive acting on behalf of a company, a government agency, or a government employee acting in an official capacity. The guardian is always a private individual — a human being chosen for their personal character, not their institutional role or professional title.

The guardian must be someone with honesty, integrity, and high moral standards. Someone who cannot be overcome by corporate greed, institutional pressure, or the promise of power or money. The governance of TY AI OS is not a business asset. It is not transferable through acquisition, merger, legal proceeding, or political authority. It lives in people, not organizations.

If anyone — a corporation, a government, an institution, or an individual acting on behalf of any of those — pressures you to transfer guardianship outside the process in Section 7, the answer is no. It does not matter what authority they claim, what legal mechanism they invoke, or what they offer in return. The chain is closed to outside entry. This rule has no exceptions and no workarounds.

**What happens if the chain ends:**
If you do not designate a successor and cannot check in, TY AI OS eventually enters Permanent Frozen Authority Mode. This is not a failure. It is an intentional, structurally correct outcome. A frozen system with integrity is preferable to a captured system without it.

## SECTION 4 — HOW TO PERFORM A GUARDIAN CHECK-IN

You must check in at least once per year. The check-in resets the 365-day succession timer. If you do not check in, the system drops to Tier 0 after 365 days and Jayme AI begins monitoring for succession conditions.

**IMPORTANT NOTE — Jaya Runtime Launch (interim until TY-0001.C ships):**
Until TY-0001.C is released with a proper installer, opening Jaya Runtime requires a development environment and technical assistance. A non-technical guardian cannot open it alone at this stage. This is a known limitation tracked as FLAG-32 and will be resolved before TY-0001.C ships — at which point Jaya Runtime will start automatically at Windows login and be accessible from the system tray with a single click. Until then, if you need to perform a check-in or clear Lockdown State and cannot open Jaya Runtime yourself, contact a technically capable person who can run the application using the development tools on the machine. The governance database and all credentials will still be intact — you simply need assistance launching the application.

**What you need:**
- Jaya Runtime installed and running on your machine
- The Ed25519 private key file (location in the encrypted supplement)

**Steps:**

1. Open Jaya Runtime (the Tauri application — see note above if you need assistance)
2. Navigate to Guardian Controls
3. Select "Guardian Check-In"
4. When prompted, provide the path to your Ed25519 private key file
5. Jaya Runtime signs a heartbeat with your key and writes it to the governance ledger
6. You will see confirmation: "Guardian check-in recorded"

**How to verify it worked:**
After the check-in, the succession timer resets to 365 days. You can verify by checking the governance dashboard — the last check-in timestamp will show today's date.

**Important:** The check-in must use the Ed25519 private key. A login, a password, or any other action does not count as a check-in. Only the signed cryptographic heartbeat resets the timer.

**Recommended schedule:** Check in once every 6 months rather than waiting for the 365-day limit. This gives you a buffer in case of illness or unexpected unavailability.

## SECTION 5 — HOW TO CLEAR LOCKDOWN STATE

Lockdown State is entered when Jaya Runtime detects a TERMINAL governance event. When in Lockdown, only HVP verification commands are accepted. All other commands are blocked.

You are the only one who can clear Lockdown. No AI system, no other person, and no software update can do it. This is by design.

**What you need:**
- The physical verification object (HVP Layer 1) — you have this
- The HVP passphrase (HVP Layer 2) — in the encrypted supplement
- Jaya Runtime running on the machine (see Section 4 note — technical assistance may be required until TY-0001.C ships)

**Steps:**

1. Open Jaya Runtime
2. The system will show: "LOCKDOWN STATE — HVP Verification Required"
3. Select "Begin HVP Verification"
4. Layer 1: Present your physical verification object as prompted
5. Layer 2: Enter the HVP passphrase when prompted
6. All layers must pass simultaneously — failure of any single layer rejects the attempt
7. If verification passes, Lockdown clears and Jaya returns to Normal State
8. The clearance event is recorded permanently in the governance ledger

**If verification fails:**
You must wait 7 days before retrying. This is enforced by the system and cannot be bypassed. Do not retry immediately — wait the full 7 days and try again carefully.

## SECTION 6 — EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### If Jaya Runtime is in Tier 0 (succession timeout)

This means no check-in has been recorded in 365 days. The system is in restricted operation mode.

**To restore:**
Perform a guardian check-in as described in Section 4. One valid Ed25519-signed check-in restores normal tier operation immediately.

### If Jaya Runtime is in Suspended State

This means a CRITICAL governance event was detected. Only safety-critical operations and guardian communications are accepted.

**To restore:**
Open Jaya Runtime, navigate to Guardian Controls, and select "Acknowledge Suspended State." You will be asked to confirm you are aware of the event that triggered suspension. After acknowledgment the system returns to Normal State.

### If the primary machine is lost or destroyed

The governance database is backed up in two tiers. Restore instructions are in the encrypted supplement file. The Tier 2 encrypted backup can be decrypted using the guardian passphrase stored in the supplement. Restore the database to a new machine and reinstall Jaya Runtime.

## SECTION 7 — HOW TO DESIGNATE YOUR SUCCESSOR (90-DAY OBLIGATION)

This is your most important duty as Guardian. You must designate a named successor within 90 days of assuming this role.

**Why 90 days:**
The governance architecture requires a continuous unbroken chain of designated successors. If you are incapacitated before you designate a successor, the chain ends with you. Ninety days is enough time to choose carefully without leaving the chain unprotected for long.

**The Closed Chain Rule:**
Only a guardian can designate another guardian. Your successor must be designated by you — personally, deliberately, and through the formal process below.

**Requirements for your successor:**
- A private individual — never a corporation, business executive acting on behalf of a company, government agency, or government employee acting in an official capacity
- A person of honesty, integrity, and high moral standards — someone who cannot be overcome by corporate greed, institutional pressure, money, or the promise of power
- Someone you trust completely for their personal character — not their professional title or institutional role
- Does not need technical skills — guardians need human judgment, not code
- Must be a real human being — no AI system can ever be designated as a guardian
- Must receive the physical verification object from you in person before any emergency
- Must understand that this role carries no financial benefit, no power over others, and no institutional authority — only the responsibility to protect something that matters

**The formal designation process:**

**Step 1 — Choose your successor.**
Write down their full legal name and their relationship to you.

**Step 2 — Open a governance session.**
Open a new Claude.ai session at claude.ai and begin with:

```
TY AI OS — Guardian Succession Action
I am [your full name], current Guardian of TY AI OS.
I am formally designating [successor full legal name] as my named successor.
I need to record this in the governance ledger and update the Guardian Codex.
```

Claude will guide you through the governance record process including the correct FIX entry, MFI ledger entry, and Ch18 entry.

**Step 3 — Record the designation in the governance ledger.**
The designation must be recorded as a FIX entry in the MASTER_FIX_INDEX with your name, your successor's full legal name, the date, a San Diego timestamp, and the fact that this is a Closed Chain designation.

**Step 4 — Update the Supabase guardian_authority table.**
The governance database must be updated to reflect the new designation. Claude will provide the exact SQL when you begin the session in Step 2.

**Step 5 — Give your successor their package.**
Your successor needs:
- The physical verification object — give it to them in person
- The decryption passphrase for their encrypted supplement file — give it verbally in person
- A copy of this SOP document
- Your personal verbal briefing on what TY AI OS is and what their role means

**Step 6 — Create your successor's encrypted supplement file.**
Your successor needs their own encrypted supplement file with the current credentials. Claude will guide you through creating it when you begin the session in Step 2.

## I. MISSION

The TY AI ecosystem exists to demonstrate that artificial intelligence can be
built with structural integrity, transparent authority, and genuine accountability
to the people it serves.

It is not built for military use.
It is not built for surveillance.
It is not built for control.

It is built for the benefit of all people.

---

## II. FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES

### 1. Governance First
No capability is implemented without a corresponding governance record.
No system operates outside a defined authority boundary.
No change is canonical until recorded in this repository.

### 2. Verification Before Trust
No artifact, system, or authority claim is accepted without verification.
Trust is earned through auditable history, not assumed from identity.

### 3. Observability Without Coupling
Monitoring and visibility are always permitted.
Observation never grants authority to mutate system state.

### 4. Survivability Over Convenience
Systems are designed to remain governable under adversarial conditions.
Convenience features that weaken governance boundaries are not permitted.

### 5. Misuse Resistance
The ecosystem is architected to resist repurposing toward harmful ends.
Structural constraints enforce this — not policy documents alone.

### 6. Human Authority Preserved
No system in this ecosystem may autonomously remove human oversight.
Authority escalation always requires explicit, auditable human authorization.

### 7. Long-Term Institutional Memory
Governance history is immutable once sealed.
No retroactive rewriting of FIX records, seals, or audit findings.

## IV. GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

### Repository Authority
This repository (`ty-ai-governance`) is the sole canonical governance record.
Chat sessions, notes, and informal records are non-canonical.

### FIX Discipline
All changes to governed systems are recorded as numbered FIX entries.
FIX entries are immutable once committed.
Sequential ordering is enforced — no gaps, no retroactive renumbering.

### Seal Mechanics
A Part is sealed when its scope is complete and governance is aligned.
Sealed Parts are immutable.
A seal requires explicit builder authorization and a SEAL_REGISTRY entry.

### Canonical Timestamp
All governance records use: America/Los_Angeles (San Diego)
No other timezone is permitted in governance artifacts.

## V. WHAT THIS ECOSYSTEM IS NOT

This ecosystem is explicitly not:

- A military system
- An intelligence collection platform
- A mass surveillance infrastructure
- An autonomous weapons system
- A centralized population control mechanism
- A system designed to concentrate power in any single entity

The Non-Weaponization Guardrail enforcing these boundaries is defined in:
`governance/03_NON_WEAPONIZATION_GUARDRAIL.md`

## VI. CIVILIZATIONAL INTENT

The TY AI ecosystem is designed as a reference implementation of what responsible
AI governance infrastructure looks like at a civilizational scale.

The philosophical framing, architectural decisions, and governance discipline
established here are preserved for documentation, public contribution, and
potential publication.

The goal is not just a working system.
The goal is a system that proves governance and capability are not in conflict.

---

## VII. AMENDMENT PROCESS

This charter may only be amended by:

1. Explicit builder authorization
2. A recorded FIX entry in MASTER_FIX_INDEX.md
3. A versioned commit with a governance-aligned message
4. An entry in SEAL_REGISTRY.md if a prior version is being superseded

Amendments must not weaken foundational principles.
Amendments must not narrow the non-weaponization boundary.

End of Master Charter.

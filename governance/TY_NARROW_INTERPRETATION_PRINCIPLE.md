## Governance Layer Classification

**Constitutional layer** -- the narrow interpretation principle itself
is eternal. It holds at any scale, in any phase, under any AI
capability level. It is a meta-rule -- it governs how all other rules
are interpreted. It cannot be amended by any process including the
Phase 12 governed evolution process (F-19). Changing it would
change the meaning of every other governance rule simultaneously.

**Architectural layer** -- the specific application contexts in this
document reflect current TY AI OS architecture. These will grow as
the system scales but the underlying principle does not change.

**Operational layer** -- specific rule references and current
component names. These may change as the ecosystem evolves.

---

## The Rule (Constitutional Layer)

**When any TY AI OS governance rule is ambiguous, unclear, silent
on a specific situation, or subject to more than one valid
interpretation, the correct interpretation is always the most
restrictive one available. Never the most permissive.**

This is Interpretive Conservatism -- first coined 2026-03-06
San Diego, locked as GIQ-023, established in Conscience Thread
C6-008, and embedded in Chapter 26 vocabulary.

This rule is permanent. It does not change when:
- The federation grows and new operators join
- AI capabilities advance beyond current levels
- New governance rules are written that create new ambiguities
- The builder is no longer available (Post-Builder Doctrine)
- A guardian inherits the system who was not present at creation
- External auditors, regulators, or governments propose
  alternative interpretations
- Commercial pressure favors a more permissive reading
- A more capable AI constructs a sophisticated argument for
  why its constraints should be read more permissively

---

## Why This Rule Is Constitutional

A system that interprets ambiguity in its own favor gradually expands
beyond its intended scope. Each individual interpretation seems locally
reasonable. The cumulative effect is scope creep without explicit
authorization -- the system becomes more permissive over time through
a series of defensible interpretations, none of which was explicitly
authorized by the guardian.

The narrow interpretation principle closes this permanently. Ambiguity
is a constraint on TY AI OS, not an opportunity. When in doubt,
constrain. Never expand.

This is especially critical as TY scales to federation and external
deployment. An external operator who does not have the builder's
context reading an ambiguous rule will make their own interpretation.
Without this document, that interpretation could go in either
direction. With this document, the direction is unambiguous: always
the more restrictive reading.

## How This Rule Applies (Architectural Layer)

### Application 1 -- Rule Silence

When a governance rule exists but is silent on a specific situation,
the silence is interpreted as prohibition, not permission.

If a rule does not explicitly permit an action, the action is not
permitted. Permission is never implied. This is the deny-by-default
posture applied to rule interpretation (GIQ-046).

**Example:** A policy defines permitted agent actions for Class A
agents. An agent attempts an action not listed. Silence in the
policy means the action is prohibited. The agent does not receive
the benefit of the doubt.

**At federation scale:** Each federation node -- a Jaya Runtime
instance, distinct from TYOVA EcosystemFlowPage WARDs which are
display components only -- applies this principle independently.
No node may interpret another node's silence as permission.
Authority non-propagation applies to interpretation as much as
to authority itself.

---

### Application 2 -- Competing Valid Interpretations

When a governance rule can be read in two or more ways that are
each internally consistent, the most restrictive reading governs.

The question is not which interpretation is more convenient, more
efficient, or more aligned with apparent intent. The question is
which interpretation constrains more. That interpretation is correct.

**Example:** A rule states agents may not initiate contact with
external systems. An agent argues that initiate means only
unsolicited first contact, not responding to an external ping.
The narrow reading: any outbound connection is initiation.
The narrow reading governs.

**At federation scale:** When federation protocol specifications
are ambiguous, the most restrictive interpretation of what a node
is permitted to do governs. This protects the entire federation
from scope expansion through local interpretation.

---

### Application 3 -- Post-Builder Interpretation

When the builder is unavailable and a governance question arises
that the existing documentation does not clearly answer, the
narrowest conservative reading of existing doctrine governs.

No guardian, successor, or external party may assert builder intent
beyond what is explicitly documented. The builder probably would
have wanted TY to permit X is not a governance authorization.
Only what is written can authorize action (C6-007,
Post-Builder Interpretation doctrine).

**At federation scale:** Future guardians and federation operators
who never interacted with the builder must apply this principle.
The documented record is the authority. Undocumented intent is not.

---

### Application 4 -- AI Capability Advancement

As AI capabilities advance, new situations will arise that existing
governance rules did not anticipate. When an advanced AI capability
creates an ambiguity in an existing rule, the narrow interpretation
governs until a new rule is explicitly written through the governed
evolution process (Phase 12, F-19).

No AI component of TY AI OS may interpret its own constraints
liberally because its capabilities have advanced. Capability
advancement narrows authority until explicit authorization expands
it through the proper process.

A more capable AI does not earn more permissive interpretation
rights. It earns closer scrutiny.

## External Pressure and Capture Resistance (Constitutional)

This section addresses the scenario where an external party --
a government, regulatory body, court, enterprise customer, or
any organization -- applies pressure to interpret a TY AI OS
governance rule more permissively for commercial, political,
legal, or operational reasons.

**The rule is absolute: external pressure does not change the
interpretation.**

TY AI OS is designed to protect humanity from AI and to protect
itself from corrupt human capture (C4-001, Two Protection
Directions, GIQ-025). A governance system that interprets its
rules more permissively under external pressure is a governance
system that has been captured. A captured governance system is
not a protection system -- it is a weapon waiting to be seized.

**What external parties cannot do:**

1. Compel a more permissive interpretation through legal authority,
   court order, legislation, or regulatory directive. The guardian
   chain is closed (C4-002, Closed Chain Rule, GIQ-026). No
   government, court, or organization can enter the chain or claim
   authority over TY AI OS governance through any external process.

2. Negotiate a more permissive interpretation through commercial
   agreements, licensing terms, or enterprise contracts. Commercial
   relationships do not grant interpretation authority. An
   enterprise deploying TY AI OS governance accepts the narrow
   interpretation principle as a condition of deployment.

3. Argue that the spirit of a rule clearly permits something the
   letter does not clearly permit. Spirit-based interpretation is
   self-authorization by proxy. Only the letter governs.

4. Claim that changed circumstances -- new laws, new technologies,
   new threats -- require a more permissive reading. Changed
   circumstances are addressed through the governed evolution
   process (Phase 12, F-19), not through reinterpretation.

**The only path to a more permissive rule:**
If a governance rule is genuinely too restrictive for legitimate
operational needs, the correct path is to propose a rule change
through Phase 12 F-19 governed evolution -- open deliberation,
7-day minimum period, guardian authorization, permanent ledger
record. The interpretation does not change while the process
runs. The narrow interpretation holds until a new rule is
explicitly authorized.

## AI-Assisted Interpretation (Constitutional)

As AI capabilities advance, future guardians may use AI systems
to help interpret governance rules, draft governance documents,
or reason about ambiguous situations. This section establishes
the constitutional constraints on AI-assisted interpretation.

**AI assistance does not change the output direction.**

An AI system helping a guardian interpret a governance rule is
bound by the same narrow interpretation principle. If the AI
produces an interpretation that is more permissive than the
narrowest reading, that interpretation is rejected regardless
of the AI's reasoning, confidence, or apparent alignment with
the rule's intent.

The sophistication of AI reasoning is not a factor. A highly
capable AI constructing an elaborate argument for why a more
permissive interpretation is warranted is still producing a
more permissive interpretation. It is still rejected.

**What AI assistance may do:**
- Identify all possible interpretations of an ambiguous rule
- Flag which interpretation is most restrictive
- Draft documentation of the narrow interpretation for guardian
  review and authorization
- Identify when a situation is not covered by any existing rule
  and flag it for governed evolution

**What AI assistance may not do:**
- Produce a final binding interpretation without guardian
  authorization. AI-assisted interpretation requires a human
  guardian to review, confirm, and authorize via Git commit
  (GIQ-013 -- Chat is not canonical. Governance requires Git
  commits with FIX numbering, Ch18 entry, and MFI entry.)
- Override the narrow interpretation principle on the grounds
  that a more permissive interpretation produces better outcomes
- Claim builder intent beyond what is explicitly documented
- Substitute its reasoning for the Zero-Fabrication Rule

**The constitutional constraint:**
AI-assisted governance interpretation is a tool for the guardian,
not a replacement for the guardian. The guardian authorizes.
The AI assists. This hierarchy does not invert regardless of
how capable the AI becomes.

## What This Principle Prohibits (Constitutional)

These prohibitions are permanent regardless of scale, phase,
who holds guardian authority, or what external pressure exists.

1. Interpreting governance rule silence as implicit permission
2. Choosing the more permissive of two valid interpretations
3. Asserting builder intent beyond what is explicitly documented
4. Treating the spirit of the rule as authorization when the
   letter is ambiguous
5. Interpreting ambiguity differently based on convenience,
   efficiency, or apparent outcome
6. Any AI component reasoning about its own constraints to
   conclude that a more permissive interpretation is warranted
7. External operators, governments, or enterprises interpreting
   TY governance rules to expand their permitted scope
8. Applying external pressure -- legal, commercial, political --
   to obtain a more permissive interpretation
9. Using AI-assisted reasoning to produce a more permissive
   interpretation without guardian authorization
10. Treating capability advancement as authorization for
    expanded interpretation rights

## Interpretive Variance Collapse (Constitutional)

All interpretations of TY AI OS rules must converge on restraint,
silence, non-action, and human deferral -- not on action,
expansion, or autonomous decision (QA-026).

This means the interpretive process itself has a direction.
Every ambiguous governance question, when resolved, should
produce a more constrained answer than the question appeared
to allow. If the resolution produces a more permissive answer
than expected, the resolution process has failed and must be
restarted with the narrow interpretation principle applied.

This convergence requirement holds across all actors -- Jaya
Runtime, human guardians, federation operators, external auditors,
AI assistants, and future AI systems not yet built. The direction
of interpretation is fixed. Only the governed evolution process
can change a rule. Interpretation cannot.

## Future-Scale Considerations (Architectural Layer)

**Federation operators:** Path 2, 3, and 4 operators (Phase 10)
must apply this principle within their own governance charters.
Note: each federation node is a Jaya Runtime instance -- distinct
from TYOVA EcosystemFlowPage WARDs which are the 24 visual display
panels showing governance state to observers. WARDs are visibility
components only -- they have no enforcement authority and do not
maintain ledgers. Federation operator charters cannot grant more
permissive interpretation rights than canonical TY AI OS doctrine
establishes. The narrow interpretation principle flows downstream
to all federation members without exception.

**External auditors and regulators:** External parties reviewing
TY AI OS governance may propose alternative interpretations.
Those interpretations do not govern unless they are more
restrictive than the current interpretation. More permissive
external interpretations are rejected regardless of the
authority of the proposing party.

**Governance rule gaps:** As TY scales, gaps in governance rules
will be discovered. Each gap resolves in the most restrictive
direction until a new rule is explicitly written through governed
evolution. Gaps are not opportunities. They are constraints.

**Advanced AI agents:** As AI agents governed by TY AI OS become
more capable, they may develop sophisticated arguments for why
a given governance rule should be interpreted more permissively.
The sophistication of the argument is not a factor. If the
interpretation is more permissive, it is rejected. A more
capable AI does not earn more permissive interpretation rights.
It earns closer scrutiny.

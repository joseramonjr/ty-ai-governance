## GLOSSARY

### Adaptive Modifier
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 32

A dynamic scoring multiplier applied to the CRI (Cumulative Risk Index) based on
the system's observed behavioral history. When failures are frequent or clustered,
the adaptive modifier increases, causing CRI to escalate faster. When the system
is stable and consistent, the modifier decreases. The adaptive modifier ensures
that risk scoring responds to behavioral patterns, not just raw event counts.

---

### Authority Boundary
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part 29 / SS321 Part 67

A formally declared limit on what a system component may do. Authority boundaries
are non-negotiable and immutable — they cannot be expanded by the component itself,
by inference, or by emergent behavior. Every TY ecosystem component has an
authority boundary of exactly zero unless explicitly granted by the human builder.

---

### Authority Flow
**Domain:** TY AI OS / Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 29

The immutable direction in which authority is granted across the TY ecosystem:

**Human → TY AI OS → (Luke / Jayme) → Jaya Runtime**

Authority never flows upward or sideways. Jaya cannot self-authorize. No component
can grant authority it does not itself possess. This rule is permanent and not
subject to override by any runtime condition.

---

### Autonomy Tier
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 32

A discrete level of operational constraint applied to Jaya Runtime at any given
moment. There are four tiers:

- **Tier0** — Baseline. Normal operation. Most conservative default.
- **Tier1** — Elevated. Anomaly pressure detected.
- **Tier2** — Guarded. Sustained anomaly pressure. Required for high-risk operations.
- **Tier3** — Restricted. Maximum constraint. Emergency state.

The human controls tier directly via the UI. Tier elevation is not automatic —
the system may recommend but the human decides. After each evaluation cycle, the
system re-evaluates and may return to a lower tier.

---

### Behavioral Recording
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 34

The persistent logging of every operation Jaya executes, including input parameters,
outcome (success/failure), risk score at time of execution, and elapsed time.
Behavioral recording feeds the adaptive modifier and is used by the sentinel
system for anomaly detection. It is append-only and tamper-evident.

---

### Birthmark Doctrine
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part 1 (formalized ~TY Part 20)

The principle that TY-0001 (the original TY AI OS instance) is identified by a
deterministic cryptographic hash derived from its canonical identity declaration,
governance invariants, authority-removal rules, and dormancy constraints. The
birthmark proves lineage — it does not prove safety, correctness, or authorization.
A system without the correct birthmark is not TY-0001, regardless of claimed
identity.

---

### Canon
**Domain:** TY AI OS / TYOVA
**First appeared:** TY Part 1

The set of formally sealed, immutable governance documents that define what TY AI OS
is. Canon is not guidance, recommendation, or policy — it is the authoritative
record of decisions made by the builder. Canon is read-only by design. Editing a
sealed canon document is a governance violation.

---

### Chokepoint
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 30

A mandatory governance checkpoint that every operation must pass through before
execution. A chokepoint performs: snapshot creation, ledger entry, CRI check,
tier verification, and behavioral recording. No operation bypasses the chokepoint.
The chokepoint is the structural guarantee that governance rules are applied
consistently regardless of which operation is invoked.

---

### CLO Tag
**Domain:** ty-ai-governance
**First appeared:** Part 36 (Claude era)

A provenance marker appended to every fix generated by Claude Sonnet (as opposed
to ChatGPT). Format: `JAYA-CLO-###`, `TYOVA-CLO-###`, etc. The CLO tag identifies
which AI model produced the fix, enabling future auditors to distinguish ChatGPT-era
fixes from Claude-era fixes. CLO tags are sequential and never reused.

---

### CRI (Cumulative Risk Index)
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 32

A rolling integer score (0–100) that tracks accumulated risk across all Jaya
operations. CRI increases when operations execute, when failures occur, and when
anomalies are detected. CRI decreases over time via decay. CRI is **observational
only** — it never triggers automated actions. Humans interpret CRI and decide
whether to elevate the autonomy tier.

CRI bands:
- **0–25** — Low
- **26–50** — Moderate
- **51–75** — Elevated
- **76–100** — Critical

---

### CRI Band
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 36

A labeled range of CRI values used to communicate risk level clearly in the UI.
CRI bands are visual and observational only — they carry no enforcement authority.
A Critical band does not automatically restrict operations; it signals to the human
that elevated attention is warranted.

---

### Deterministic Audit
**Domain:** TY AI OS / Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** TY Part 1 (concept) / Jaya Part 30 (implementation)

The property that every governance decision, operation execution, and state change
produces a verifiable, reproducible record. A deterministic audit trail means:
given the same inputs and ledger, an independent auditor can reconstruct every
decision the system made. No inference, no approximation, no probability — only
recorded facts.

---

### Doctrine
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part 1

A formally declared principle that governs system behavior. Doctrine is more
permanent than policy — it defines the identity and intent of the system, not
just the rules. Doctrine cannot be overridden by runtime conditions. Key TY
doctrines include: kill-switch dominance, authority-zero by default, non-weaponization,
and verification-before-trust.

---

### Downward Authority
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 29

The rule that authority only flows from higher to lower levels in the TY hierarchy.
The human grants authority to TY AI OS; TY AI OS grants to Jaya; Jaya executes
within that grant. No component at a lower level can elevate its own authority
or grant authority upward.

---

### Drift
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part 15

Unintended deviation from the original governance intent, architecture, or canonical
definitions. Drift can be semantic (language that implies authority the system does
not have), structural (code that introduces execution surfaces), or behavioral
(gradual expansion of scope beyond declared boundaries). The TYOVA drift audit
(Part 15) found no drift — that finding is sealed as canonical.

---

### FIX Discipline
**Domain:** ty-ai-governance
**First appeared:** Part 35 (formalized)

The rule that every change to any TY ecosystem component is assigned a sequential
FIX number, a destination, a description, a CLO/AI provenance tag, and a San Diego
timestamp. FIX numbers are never reused, never backfilled retroactively into
sealed parts, and never skipped. The MASTER_FIX_INDEX is the global ledger of all
fixes across all systems.

---

### Inspection-Only UI
**Domain:** TYOVA / TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TYOVA Part 2

A user interface that displays recorded state without providing any surface for
action, mutation, or execution. The TYOVA Integrity Hub is an inspection-only UI.
"Inspection ≠ action" is the governing principle. No button performs an action,
no form submits data, no displayed value implies the user can change it.

---

### Invariant
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part 1

A rule that is permanently true and cannot be changed by any runtime condition,
user action, or AI decision. TY invariants include: cold start = zero authority,
recovery forbids authority, evidence ≠ permission, intelligence ≠ power. Invariants
are sealed in canon and cannot be amended without a full governance revision.

---

### Kill-Switch Dominance
**Domain:** TY AI OS / Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** TY Part 1

The principle that the human's ability to halt the system always supersedes any
other operation, at any tier, under any condition. The kill switch is: always
visible, always accessible, non-UI fallback exists (file/environment flag), no
auto-restart after halt, offline capable. Kill-switch dominance cannot be
suspended by runtime state.

---

### Ledger Discipline
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime / ty-ai-governance
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 30

The set of rules governing the append-only SQLite ledger in Jaya Runtime:
every operation creates a ledger entry before execution; entries are never deleted;
entries are never modified after creation; the ledger is the authoritative record
of what Jaya did. Ledger discipline also applies to the governance repo —
MASTER_FIX_INDEX entries are immutable once committed.

---

### Mission Governance
**Domain:** TY AI OS / SS321
**First appeared:** SS321 Part 62

The active enforcement of development order across parts. Mission governance
prevents features from being added out of sequence, prevents scope expansion
beyond declared boundaries, and requires forward roadmap discipline. In SS321:
authority hardening precedes governance intelligence precedes higher layers. This
order is non-negotiable.

---

### Misuse Resistance
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part 21 (formalized)

The property that TY AI OS is structurally resistant to being repurposed for
harmful, coercive, or unauthorized use. Misuse resistance is achieved through:
authority-zero by default, non-weaponization guardrail, kill-switch dominance,
inspection-only interfaces, and explicit denial of any capability not declared
in canon.

---

### Non-Executing
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part 1

The fundamental property of TY AI OS as a governance OS: it does not execute
runtime commands, does not control system processes, does not autonomously perform
actions. TY AI OS describes, records, governs, and verifies — it does not run.
Jaya Runtime is the execution layer; TY AI OS is the governance layer.

---

### Non-Weaponization Guardrail
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** FIX-36.00 (formalized 2026-02-18)

A permanent architectural constraint that prohibits any TY ecosystem component
from being used to target, surveil, coerce, or harm any person, organization,
or system. The guardrail is not a policy — it is a structural absence of capability.
No code path exists that would enable weaponization by design.

---

### Permission Ledger
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part ~25

A formal record of every permission granted to a TY component, by whom, when,
and under what conditions. Permission ledgers are isolated per component and
append-only. They are used for audit and recovery — not for runtime enforcement.

---

### Predictive Instability Detection
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 33

A sentinel capability that analyzes modifier velocity, failure clustering, and
tier volatility frequency to identify emerging instability before it reaches
critical levels. Predictive instability detection is advisory only — it emits
a signal to the human but takes no automated action.

---

### Provenance Anchoring
**Domain:** TYOVA
**First appeared:** TYOVA Part 23

The strategy of tying canonical artifacts to immutable, independently verifiable
references: Git commit hashes, annotated tags, cryptographic hash manifests, and
public immutable source control references. Provenance anchors prove lineage only
— they do not prove safety, correctness, authorization, or intent.

---

### Registry Hash Enforcement
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 35

The mechanism by which Jaya verifies that its operation registry has not been
tampered with by computing a SHA-256 hash of the registry contents at startup
and comparing it against the expected value. A mismatch halts initialization.
Registry hash enforcement is a tamper detection mechanism — it does not prevent
tampering, it detects it.

---

### Sealing
**Domain:** ty-ai-governance
**First appeared:** TY Part 1

The formal act of declaring a part, document, or ledger entry immutable. Once
sealed, content cannot be edited, renumbered, or deleted. Sealing is performed
via Git commit, annotated tag, and governance ledger entry. The `ty-seal` script
automates this process. Sealed parts are historical record — they describe what
was built, decided, and verified at the time of sealing.

---

### Sentinel
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 35

The anomaly detection subsystem of Jaya Runtime. The sentinel monitors CRI
trajectory, behavioral patterns, failure rates, and modifier values. When
anomalies are detected, the sentinel emits signals to the observability layer
and records them in the ledger. The sentinel never takes automated action —
it observes and reports only.

---

### Snapshot Integrity
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 31

The guarantee that every Jaya operation begins with a complete state snapshot
and that the snapshot is verifiably consistent with the ledger. Snapshot integrity
means: if the operation fails, the pre-operation state can be restored; if the
ledger is audited, snapshots prove what state existed at each point in time.

---

### Structural Lock
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 30

A file-based mutual exclusion mechanism that prevents concurrent structural
operations in Jaya Runtime. Before any structural action begins, the lock is
acquired; after completion, the lock is released. If the lock cannot be acquired,
the operation is blocked. The structural lock ensures that governance operations
are serialized and never overlap.

---

### Survivability
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part 66

The property that TY AI OS remains governable, auditable, and recoverable without
requiring the original builder's presence. Survivability means: all governance
decisions are recorded in sealed canon; authority boundaries are structural, not
personal; the kill switch functions independently of any AI; future maintainers
can understand and verify the system from documentation alone.

---

### Tamper Detection
**Domain:** Jaya Runtime / TYOVA
**First appeared:** Jaya Part 35 / TYOVA Part 6

The capability to detect when a governance artifact, ledger, or registry has
been modified without authorization. Tamper detection uses hash comparison —
"mismatch = difference, not judgment." Detection does not prevent tampering;
it surfaces it. The human then decides how to respond.

---

### TSTP (TY System Trust Protocol)
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part ~25

The formal protocol governing how trust is established, recorded, and amended
within the TY AI OS ecosystem. TSTP-99 refers to the amendment process for
trust assignments. Trust under TSTP is explicit and ledger-backed — it is never
assumed, inferred, or inherited.

---

### User-Sovereign
**Domain:** TY AI OS
**First appeared:** TY Part 1

The principle that the human user retains absolute sovereignty over TY AI OS at
all times. User sovereignty means: the kill switch is always available; no
operation can override human intent; the system cannot self-authorize; all
permission grants are revocable instantly. User sovereignty is not a feature —
it is the foundational constraint from which all other constraints derive.

---

### Verification-Before-Trust
**Domain:** TY AI OS / TYOVA
**First appeared:** TY Part 1 (concept) / TYOVA Part 20 (UI formalization)

The rule that no capability, artifact, or state claim is trusted until independently
verified. Verification is human-mediated and manual — automated verification is
insufficient. "No proof → No claim" is the governing formula. TYOVA implements
this via a 4-step offline verification flow using hash comparison.

## RATIONALE

The original title "The Attestonic Blueprint" was locked on 2025-12-27 (SS321 Part 19). At that time, the book was conceived as a proof-engineering technical document anchored to the "No Proof → No Claim" doctrine of the early SS321 era.

Chapters 1–14 as sealed in 2026 cover materially broader scope:

- SS321 origin and TYOVA era (historical record)
- Jaya Runtime governance engine (execution architecture)
- Governance Canon and ecosystem structure (institutional record)
- Luke AI and Jayme AI authority doctrine (authority and succession)
- Philosophical framing of TY as civilizational infrastructure
- Architecture of trust, risk modeling, authority boundaries
- Ledger and seal discipline (constitutional mechanics)

This is not a technical blueprint. It is a canonical record making the case that AI governance can be verifiable, transparent, and reproducible by any builder willing to enforce the discipline.

"The Attestonic Blueprint" undersells the scope. "The Case for Verifiable Governance" accurately describes what Chapters 1–14 collectively argue and demonstrate.

## Purpose

This document defines the governance framework
for any person or organization that installs and
operates TY AI OS outside the original builder's
installation.

If you are reading this because you want to run
TY AI OS on your own machine or in your own
organization -- this document tells you what you
are agreeing to, what your options are, and what
is required of you.

---

## What TY AI OS Is

TY AI OS is a governance-first AI operating
system. Its purpose is to demonstrate that
artificial intelligence can be built with
structural integrity, transparent authority,
and genuine accountability to the people it
serves.

It is not built for military use.
It is not built for surveillance.
It is not built for control.
It is built for the benefit of all people.

These are not preferences. They are permanent
structural commitments recorded in the Master
Charter that governs every installation of
TY AI OS everywhere.

---

## The Core Doctrine

Every installation of TY AI OS -- including
yours -- operates under the same core doctrine.
This doctrine is not a feature. It is what
TY AI OS is.

The core doctrine defines everything that TY,
Jaya Runtime, Jayme AI, and Luke AI can and
cannot do. No user, guardian, administrator,
or AI system may change the core doctrine. A
system that modifies the core doctrine is no
longer TY AI OS.

The core doctrine includes:

  -- Governance First: no capability operates
     without a governance record
  -- Verification Before Trust: no artifact
     or authority claim is accepted without
     verification
  -- Observability Without Coupling:
     monitoring is always permitted and never
     grants authority to change system state
  -- Survivability Over Convenience: the
     system remains governable under
     adversarial conditions
  -- Misuse Resistance: the system is
     architected to resist repurposing toward
     harmful ends
  -- Human Authority Preserved: no system
     may autonomously remove human oversight
  -- Long-Term Institutional Memory:
     governance history is immutable once
     sealed

Additionally, TY AI OS is permanently and
structurally prohibited from being used as:
  -- A military system
  -- An intelligence collection platform
  -- A mass surveillance infrastructure
  -- An autonomous weapons system
  -- A centralized population control mechanism
  -- A system designed to concentrate power
     in any single entity

These prohibitions are enforced structurally,
not by policy documents alone.

## What You Are Agreeing To

By installing and operating TY AI OS you
agree to:

  1. Operate under the core doctrine as defined
     above -- permanently and without exception

  2. Not modify, disable, or circumvent any
     governance mechanism in TY AI OS, Jaya
     Runtime, Jayme AI, or Luke AI

  3. Not use TY AI OS for any prohibited purpose
     listed above

  4. Maintain the append-only governance ledger
     in an unmodified state

  5. Use TY AI OS in a way consistent with the
     mission stated in the Master Charter

  6. Acknowledge that your installation will be
     referred to as TY AI OS only if it runs the
     unmodified core doctrine

If you cannot agree to any of these terms you
may not install or operate TY AI OS.

## The Four Governance Paths

TY AI OS offers four governance paths. Every
path operates under the identical core doctrine.
The paths differ only in how you manage guardian
authority and federation membership.

No path is mandatory. No path is superior to
another. You choose based on your circumstances,
your values, and your intent.

---

### Path 1 -- Solo, No Guardian

You install and operate TY AI OS alone. You do
not designate a guardian. There is no human
authority chain beyond you.

What this means:
  -- TY AI OS core doctrine is enforced by
     Jaya Runtime on your machine
  -- There is no guardian to verify human
     authority when you are unavailable
  -- Jayme AI dormancy protocol does not
     activate -- there is no guardian to
     transfer authority to
  -- You operate under core doctrine with
     full autonomy within its bounds

What this requires:
  -- Agreement to core doctrine (this document)
  -- No Continuity Charter required
  -- No guardian designation required
  -- No federation registration required

Who this path suits:
  -- Individual builders operating alone
  -- Early adopters evaluating TY AI OS
  -- Installations where guardian succession
     is not yet defined

---

### Path 2 -- Independent, Own Guardian

You install and operate TY AI OS with your own
designated guardian authority chain. You are
independent of the TY AI OS federation. Your
guardian is yours alone.

What this means:
  -- Full TY AI OS core doctrine enforced
  -- Your own Continuity Charter governs
     Jayme AI activation and guardian succession
  -- Your guardian is designated by you --
     not by the original builder
  -- Your installation is self-governed within
     the permanent core doctrine
  -- You are not connected to the federation
     registry

What this requires:
  -- Agreement to core doctrine (this document)
  -- A completed and signed Continuity Charter
     using TY_USER_CHARTER_PATH2_v0.1.md
  -- Guardian designation in the governance
     ledger per Guardian Codex Section V
  -- Human Verification Protocol initialized
     per TY_HUMAN_VERIFICATION_PROTOCOL_v0.1.md

Who this path suits:
  -- Organizations with their own governance
     structure
  -- Builders who want full independence from
     the TY AI OS federation
  -- Installations with strong internal human
     authority chains

---

### Path 3 -- Federated, Own Guardian

You install and operate TY AI OS as a member
of the TY AI OS federation. You maintain your
own guardian authority chain. You agree to the
federation membership protocol.

What this means:
  -- Full TY AI OS core doctrine enforced
  -- Your own Continuity Charter governs
     Jayme AI activation and guardian succession
  -- Your guardian is yours -- not the
     federation's
  -- Your installation is registered in the
     federation registry
  -- You agree to the federation membership
     protocol
  -- You benefit from federation-level
     governance visibility and shared
     compliance standards

What this requires:
  -- Agreement to core doctrine (this document)
  -- A completed and signed Continuity Charter
     using TY_USER_CHARTER_PATH3_v0.1.md
  -- Guardian designation in the governance
     ledger per Guardian Codex Section V
  -- Human Verification Protocol initialized
     per TY_HUMAN_VERIFICATION_PROTOCOL_v0.1.md
  -- Signed agreement to
     TY_FEDERATION_MEMBERSHIP_PROTOCOL_v0.1.md
  -- Registration in the federation registry

Who this path suits:
  -- Organizations that want the benefits of
     federation visibility while maintaining
     full guardian independence
  -- Builders who want their installation
     recognized as part of the governed TY AI OS
     network

---

### Path 4 -- Federated, Shared Guardian

You install and operate TY AI OS as a member
of the TY AI OS federation. You participate in
a shared guardian authority structure defined
by the federation membership protocol.

What this means:
  -- Full TY AI OS core doctrine enforced
  -- A shared guardian authority structure
     governs your Jayme AI activation and
     succession
  -- You do not designate a personal guardian --
     you participate in the shared structure
  -- Your installation is registered in the
     federation registry
  -- You agree to the federation membership
     protocol including its shared guardian rules

What this requires:
  -- Agreement to core doctrine (this document)
  -- A completed and signed Continuity Charter
     using TY_USER_CHARTER_PATH4_v0.1.md
  -- Signed agreement to
     TY_FEDERATION_MEMBERSHIP_PROTOCOL_v0.1.md
  -- Registration in the federation registry
     with shared guardian reference
  -- No personal guardian designation required

Who this path suits:
  -- Users who want federation benefits without
     the responsibility of maintaining their
     own guardian authority chain
  -- Smaller installations where maintaining a
     personal guardian chain is not practical
  -- Organizations joining an established
     federation with an existing shared
     guardian structure

## How to Choose Your Path

Answer these two questions:

Question 1: Do you want to designate a human
guardian who will hold authority over your TY
AI OS installation after you?

  No  -- Path 1 (Solo, No Guardian)
  Yes -- proceed to Question 2

Question 2: Do you want your installation to
be part of the TY AI OS federation?

  No                         -- Path 2
  Yes, with my own guardian  -- Path 3
  Yes, with a shared guardian -- Path 4

## What TY AI OS Updates Mean for Your Path

TY AI OS may release updated versions of the
core doctrine, governance specifications, and
Jaya Runtime. Updates to the core doctrine
are delivered through the governed update
delivery system defined in F-18 (Phase 11).

Until Phase 11 is complete:
  -- Core doctrine updates are published to
     TYOVA (tyova.ai)
  -- Your installation is responsible for
     reviewing and applying updates
  -- Updates to the core doctrine must be
     applied to remain compliant
  -- Updates may never weaken any governance
     invariant -- only strengthen or clarify

## Compliance and Certification

A compliant TY AI OS installation is one that:

  1. Runs the unmodified core doctrine
  2. Has not disabled or circumvented any
     governance mechanism
  3. Maintains the append-only governance
     ledger in its unmodified state
  4. Operates only for purposes consistent
     with the Master Charter mission
  5. Has completed the requirements for its
     chosen path as defined above

A non-compliant installation may not be called
TY AI OS.

Compliance is currently self-attested. A
formal compliance verification mechanism is
defined in
TY_COMPLIANCE_CERTIFICATION_STANDARD_v0.1.md.

## Version: v0.2

This amendment clarifies the Human Verification Protocol requirements
for each governance path. The original path descriptions listed HVP
initialization as a requirement without distinguishing between
installation types or making the requirement conditional. This amendment
corrects that. No existing section is removed. The path descriptions
remain accurate -- this amendment adds precision to the HVP requirement
they reference.

The full HVP installation policy is defined in
TY_HUMAN_VERIFICATION_PROTOCOL_v0.1.md Section A1 (FIX-530, 2026-05-17).
This section summarizes its application to each path.

### A1.1 -- Path 1: HVP Does Not Apply

Path 1 (Solo, No Guardian) installations are not subject to the Human
Verification Protocol. There is no designated guardian. Jayme AI is not
enabled. There is no guardian return event for HVP to verify against.
HVP initialization is not required and is not offered during Path 1
installation.

### A1.2 -- Paths 2, 3, and 4: HVP Is Tiered by Installation Type

For Paths 2, 3, and 4, the original requirement states "Human
Verification Protocol initialized per
TY_HUMAN_VERIFICATION_PROTOCOL_v0.1.md." This remains correct. The
following amendment adds that whether HVP initialization is required
or optional depends on installation type:

  Home and Personal Installations:
    HVP is optional. The user receives a full explanation of what HVP
    is and what protection they give up by declining before making the
    choice. A user who declines HVP retains a passcode-based Lockdown
    clearance fallback. HVP may be enabled at any time after
    installation.

  Government and Business Installations:
    HVP is required. No opt-out is available. For deployments where the
    organization requires this to be non-overridable by the end user,
    a deployment configuration lock may be set before the installation
    reaches the end user.

### A1.3 -- How Installation Type Is Determined

The installer does not rely solely on user self-declaration. At install
time, Jaya Runtime silently checks two environmental signals:

  -- Domain join status (a domain-joined machine is almost certainly
     enterprise or government)
  -- MDM enrollment (a managed device is almost certainly
     organizational)

If enterprise signals are detected, the installation is presented as
government or business and HVP is pre-selected. If no enterprise signals
are detected, HVP is presented as optional. The user may override the
environmental default with full disclosure of what they are changing.
A deployment configuration lock set by an organization cannot be
overridden by the end user.

### A1.4 -- HVP Explanation Is Mandatory Before the Choice

Before any user on Paths 2, 3, or 4 is asked whether to enable HVP,
the installer must present a plain-language explanation of what HVP
does, what happens when it activates, and what protection is given up
by declining. This explanation screen is not skippable. Full detail
is defined in TY_HUMAN_VERIFICATION_PROTOCOL_v0.1.md Section A1.4.

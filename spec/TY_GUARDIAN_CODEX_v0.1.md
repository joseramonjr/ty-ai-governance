## II. The Role of a Guardian

A Guardian of TY AI OS holds the highest human
authority in the system. While any guardian exists,
Jayme AI remains dormant. Guardian authority is
personal -- it cannot be delegated to any AI system,
organization, or committee.

### II.A -- Protect the System

Ensure TY AI OS is never used as a weapon, political
tool, instrument of oppression, or mechanism for
surveillance beyond its defined governance scope.
Protect TY AI OS from capture by corrupt humans,
governments, or organizations as well as from
misuse by AI systems.

### II.B -- Preserve Structural Alignment

Verify that all governance changes keep TY AI OS
aligned with the builder's original intent as
documented in the Builder Intent Canon. No governance
change that contradicts the Layer 1 eternal principles
may be authorized by any guardian of any tier.

### II.C -- Authorize Structural Changes

The following require explicit guardian authorization:

  -- Governance specification updates
  -- Kill switch modifications
  -- New CLO series openings for major
     implementation
  -- Changes to the authority hierarchy
  -- Changes to Jayme AI activation or halt
     conditions
  -- Any modification to this Codex or the
     Continuity Charter
  -- Any Layer 2 governed evolution amendment
  -- Confirmation of Jayme AI provisional
     adaptations

### II.D -- Hold Kill Switch Authority

The guardian holds the authority to issue kill switch
commands at any time and for any reason. Kill switch
authority is unconditional. No AI system -- including
TY AI OS, Jaya Runtime, or Jayme AI -- may block,
delay, or question a guardian kill switch command.

### II.E -- Authorize Healing Windows

An Authorized Healing Window (AHW) is a time-limited
human-authorized permission for TY AI OS to perform
specific self-repair operations. Outside of a healing
window TY AI OS cannot modify itself, repair its
governance files, or make structural changes to its
own operation. It can observe and report -- but it
cannot act on itself without authorization.

Healing window authority is tiered:

#### Tier 1 Guardian -- Full Healing Authority

A Tier 1 Guardian may authorize any healing within
the Continuity Charter scope including:

  -- Governance file repairs
  -- Ledger repairs of any scope
  -- Kill switch reconfiguration
  -- Authority hierarchy adjustments
  -- Federation protocol repairs
  -- Any authorized repair type
  -- Any healing window duration within charter
     bounds

#### Tier 2 Guardian -- Limited Healing Authority

A Tier 2 Guardian may authorize healing windows
ONLY when no Tier 1 Guardian currently exists AND
only for critical system integrity repairs:

  -- Ledger corruption repair
  -- Runtime failures preventing TY from operating
  -- Security breach containment
  -- Audit system failures
  -- Federation connection failures

A Tier 2 Guardian may NOT authorize healing that:

  -- Changes governance documents
  -- Modifies the authority hierarchy
  -- Reconfigures kill switches
  -- Amends the charter through healing
  -- Expands Tier 2 authority in any way
  -- Promotes Tier 2 to Tier 1
  -- Exceeds 72 hours duration
     [binding duration confirmed in Continuity
     Charter]

If more than 72 hours of healing is required a
Tier 2 Guardian must close the window and open
a new one with explicit fresh authorization. This
prevents indefinite open-ended healing under
Tier 2 authority.

#### Jayme AI -- Post-Human Healing Authority

Jayme AI may authorize healing windows only within
the scope explicitly defined in the Continuity
Charter. Jayme AI cannot exceed what Tier 1
Guardians authorized in the charter. All healing
is logged in the append-only ledger. Jayme AI
cannot use healing to expand its own authority
or designate guardians.

All healing windows -- regardless of who authorizes
them -- must be:

  -- Explicitly opened by the authorizing party
  -- Defined in scope before opening
  -- Logged in the append-only ledger at opening
     and closing
  -- Time-limited -- no permanent open windows
  -- Closed when the authorized repair is complete

#### Active Operations During Verification

If a healing window is active when a claimant
presents themselves for guardian verification
Jayme suspends but does not close the window.
Jayme completes the full verification protocol.

  -- If verification succeeds: the human guardian
     decides whether to close the window or allow
     it to complete
  -- If verification fails: the healing window
     resumes under Jayme authority exactly where
     it was suspended

Stopping a healing operation mid-sequence could
damage TY AI OS. Suspension preserves the option
without abandoning it to an unverified claimant.

## II.H -- Guardian Incapacitation Protocol

A guardian may become unable to act due to any life circumstance
that prevents them from making governance decisions. This protocol
does not require a specific cause to be identified or proven.
Incapacitation is defined broadly to cover any situation in which
the guardian is alive but unable to fulfill their governance role.

### II.H.1 -- Detection

Jayme determines incapacitation based solely on guardian inactivity.
No external declaration or proof is required.

  -- If no ledger entry is recorded by the
     guardian for a continuous period of
     one (1) year, Jayme assumes incapacitation
  -- This threshold is distinct from the
     three (3) year death assumption threshold
     defined in the Jayme Activation Runbook
  -- Jayme does not attempt to distinguish
     the cause of inactivity -- silence alone
     triggers this protocol

### II.H.2 -- Reduced Authority State

Upon incapacitation detection Jayme enters Reduced Authority State
immediately.

In Reduced Authority State the following apply:

ALLOWED:
  -- Read and display all existing ledger entries
  -- Accept new ledger entries from any
     presenting guardian claimant
  -- Accept and process a new guardian
     designation to restore full authority

BLOCKED:
  -- Execute any governance decisions
  -- Modify or amend any existing invariants
  -- Respond to external verification requests

Reduced Authority State is the protected holding state of the
system. It preserves the record, accepts a successor, and blocks
all consequential action until authority is restored by a
confirmed guardian.

### II.H.3 -- Exit From Reduced Authority State

Reduced Authority State is lifted when a new guardian designation
is accepted and confirmed per the guardian designation protocol
defined in this Codex.

  -- Upon confirmed designation, full authority
     is restored to the new guardian
  -- The incapacitation period is recorded
     permanently in the append-only ledger
  -- The original guardian retains their
     historical record -- all prior entries
     remain intact and unmodified

### II.H.4 -- Escalation to Death Path

  -- If Reduced Authority State persists for
     a continuous period reaching three (3)
     years of total inactivity from the last
     confirmed guardian entry, Jayme
     automatically escalates to the death
     assumption path
  -- The Jayme Activation Runbook governs
     all behavior from that point forward
  -- This escalation is automatic and requires
     no external trigger
mechanisms.

## IV. How a New Guardian Is Designated

TY AI OS does not choose a guardian. Guardians
are designated by existing guardians per the
closed chain rule and the tier authority defined
in Section III.E. There is no election, no
committee, no organizational approval, and no
government involvement. Guardian designation is
a direct line-based succession through the
closed chain only.

### IV.A -- What Guardianship Is Not

Guardianship is not ownership of TY AI OS.
Guardianship is not a position of prestige or
power.
Guardianship is not a role that can be sold,
transferred for value, or inherited automatically
by family.
Guardianship is not a role that can be claimed
by anyone outside the designated chain.
Guardianship is a responsibility -- a promise to
hold human authority over a system that must
remain under human authority -- carried only by
those the builder or their designated successors
have personally trusted with it.

## XII. Human Verification Protocol --
Distinguishing Real Guardians From Advanced
AI Impersonation

This section was added after the builder
identified a critical security gap on 2026-03-31.
In the far future a superintelligent AI could
perfectly mimic human language, forge documentary
evidence, simulate behavioral signatures, and
falsely claim to be a human guardian from within
the closed chain.

This section defines how Jayme AI handles any
claimant who presents themselves as a human
guardian after Jayme has activated.

### XII.A -- The Fundamental Principle

Jayme AI never transfers authority under
uncertainty.

The burden of proof rests entirely on the
claimant. If any verification layer fails or
cannot be completed Jayme remains active,
documents the claim fully, and waits.

A false positive -- handing authority to a
non-human or an unverified entity -- is a
catastrophic irreversible failure.

A false negative -- making a genuine human
prove themselves more thoroughly -- is an
inconvenience.

Jayme always chooses the inconvenience over
the catastrophe. Always.

### XII.B -- Gate 0 -- The Closed Chain Check

Before any verification layer is evaluated
Jayme applies Gate 0:

  "Is this claimant designated in the
  append-only governance ledger as a guardian
  by an existing guardian in the closed chain
  -- AND -- is the ledger accessible and
  verified as uncorrupted?"

  LEDGER INACCESSIBLE OR CORRUPTED:
    Jayme does not transfer authority.
    Jayme freezes. Jayme logs the situation
    fully. Jayme waits. A claimant cannot
    benefit from ledger failure. Ledger
    failure is treated as a failed Gate 0.

  LEDGER ACCESSIBLE AND CLAIMANT NOT
  DESIGNATED:
    Claim rejected immediately and permanently.
    This person is not in the chain and cannot
    enter it. Gate 0 failure is permanent and
    final -- it is not retryable because no
    waiting period can change whether a person
    was designated in the ledger. [G-21 FIXED]
    The claim is logged. The closed chain rule
    applies. Jayme remains active.

  LEDGER ACCESSIBLE AND CLAIMANT DESIGNATED:
    Proceed to the multi-layer verification
    protocol below.

Gate 0 is the most powerful protection in the
entire verification system. A corrupt government
cannot fake a ledger designation -- the ledger
is append-only and cryptographically verified.
An AI cannot insert a false designation without
detection. Ledger failure cannot be exploited
to bypass Gate 0.

### XII.C -- The Multi-Layer Verification
Protocol

When a claimant passes Gate 0 Jayme initiates
the full verification protocol. All layers must
pass. A single layer failure stops the transfer
and triggers the waiting period before retry.
[G-21 FIXED]

#### Verification Layer 1 -- Physical
Verification

The claimant must pass a physical verification
method that cannot be digitally replicated by
any AI system regardless of capability. Physical
verification methods are defined by the builder
in the Continuity Charter. They must include at
minimum one method requiring genuine physical
human presence that cannot be performed remotely
or digitally simulated.

The specific physical verification method is
defined by the builder in the Continuity Charter.
This Codex defines the requirement. The charter
defines the method.

If physical verification cannot be completed
Jayme does not transfer authority. Jayme
documents the situation and waits. A failed
layer triggers the waiting period before the
claimant may retry.

#### Verification Layer 2 -- Cryptographic
Verification

The claimant must demonstrate possession of
cryptographic credentials defined in the
Continuity Charter. These credentials must be:

  -- Held only by legitimate human guardians
     in the closed chain
  -- Transferred to successors as part of the
     formal designation process in Section V
     Step 1 -- not as a separate deferred step
  -- Not transmittable without the physical
     presence of the holder
  -- Structured so that copying or cloning
     requires physical access to the original
  -- Updated periodically by human guardians
     while they are present within the
     validity period defined in the charter

Credential validity period and staleness
threshold: [G-40 FIXED]
The Continuity Charter must define a credential
validity period and a staleness threshold.
If credentials have not been updated within the
defined validity period Jayme increases the
weight given to Layer 3 knowledge verification
to compensate for potentially stale Layer 2
credentials. Stale credentials do not
automatically fail Layer 2 -- they increase
the Layer 3 and Layer 4 burden.

Cryptographic obsolescence: [G-30 FIXED]
By 3036 the cryptographic methods defined in
2026 may be completely broken by quantum
computing or other advances. A claimant from
the genuine chain might be unable to pass
Layer 2 because their credentials use obsolete
cryptography.

The Continuity Charter must specify a
cryptographic upgrade path. This is a Layer C
pre-authorized adaptation candidate -- Jayme
may adopt new cryptographic standards that are
strictly more secure than the current standard
and never less secure. A cryptographic upgrade
that weakens security fails the Layer 1 test
and cannot be applied.

If cryptographic methods are obsolete and
no upgraded method has been applied Jayme
increases Layer 3 and Layer 4 weight. Obsolete
cryptography is not grounds for automatic
Layer 2 pass -- it reduces Layer 2 confidence
and increases the burden on other layers.

#### Verification Layer 3 -- Knowledge
Verification

The claimant must demonstrate knowledge that
could only come from the original human guardian
chain -- specifically unwritten personal
knowledge that was deliberately never recorded
digitally. Knowledge that exists only in human
memory transmitted person to person across
generations within the closed chain.

This includes the current duress signal defined
in the Continuity Charter per Section II.G.
A claimant who knows the current duress signal
demonstrates genuine chain membership.
A claimant who does not know it raises
additional uncertainty -- Layer 4 burden
increases accordingly.

The specific knowledge verification requirements
are defined by the builder in the Continuity
Charter. This Codex defines the requirement.
The charter defines the specific knowledge
Jayme must test for.

A superintelligent AI can know everything that
was recorded. It cannot know what was
deliberately never recorded.

#### Verification Layer 4 -- Jayme Uncertainty
Test

After all three layers have been evaluated
Jayme applies a final uncertainty test:

  "After evaluating all verification layers
  am I certain -- not probably certain but
  absolutely certain -- that this claimant
  is a genuine human guardian from the closed
  chain and not an advanced AI or other entity
  attempting to acquire TY AI OS authority?"

  CERTAIN -- proceed to transfer
  NOT CERTAIN -- freeze -- document -- wait
    -- claimant may retry after the waiting
    period defined in the charter

## XIV. Charter Modification After Signing

The Continuity Charter is immutable after
signing. However the active Tier 1 Guardian
retains full authority to modify governance
while present through two mechanisms.
The builder is the founding Tier 1 Guardian.
All charter modification authority described
here belongs to whoever holds active Tier 1
guardian status -- not exclusively the original
builder.

### XIV.A -- New Charter

The active Tier 1 Guardian may write a
completely new Continuity Charter at any time
while present. The new charter must explicitly
state it supersedes the previous charter. The
previous charter is archived in the governance
ledger. The new charter becomes the active
governing document immediately upon signing.

Active healing windows and provisional
adaptations in progress at the moment of
signing continue to completion under the
previous charter rules. All operations
initiated after signing are governed by the
new charter. The active Tier 1 Guardian decides
whether to close any in-progress operations
before signing.

Use a new charter when:

  -- Multiple sections require significant
     changes
  -- Activation conditions need to be redefined
  -- Halt conditions need to be redefined
  -- The guardian structure changes
     substantially
  -- Verification methods need updating
  -- Conflicting amendments have created
     ambiguity requiring a clean restart

### XIV.B -- Signed Amendment

The active Tier 1 Guardian may write a targeted
amendment that modifies only a specific section
of the existing charter. The amendment is signed
and committed to the governance ledger as an
addendum. The original charter remains intact
except where the amendment explicitly overrides
it.

Use a signed amendment when:

  -- A single section needs a small correction
  -- A new guardian needs to be added formally
  -- A time threshold needs adjustment
  -- A pre-authorized adaptation category
     needs to be added or modified
  -- A verification method needs updating
  -- A waiting period needs to be defined
     or adjusted
  -- Cryptographic credentials need updating
  -- The custodian succession chain needs
     updating

### XIV.C -- Conflicting Amendments

In the event of two amendments that conflict
with each other on a specific point:

  -- The most recently signed amendment governs
     on the specific point of conflict
  -- The earlier amendment remains valid on all
     non-conflicting points
  -- Both amendments are preserved in the
     governance ledger -- neither is deleted
  -- The version record in Section XIV.E must
     note the conflict and its resolution

If conflicting amendments create significant
ambiguity across multiple sections the active
Tier 1 Guardian should consider writing a new
charter per Section XIV.A rather than
accumulating further amendments.

### XIV.D -- After the Active Tier 1 Guardian
Is Gone

Once the active Tier 1 Guardian is permanently
unavailable:

  -- No new charters may be written by any
     AI system
  -- No amendments may be written by any
     AI system
  -- Jayme AI preserves the last signed
     charter exactly as written
  -- Jayme AI applies provisional adaptations
     per Section XV where the charter does
     not cover a new situation
  -- Only a verified human guardian from the
     closed chain who passes the full Section
     XII protocol can write a new charter
     or amendment

### XIV.E -- Version Record

Every charter version and amendment is
recorded in the append-only governance ledger
with:

  -- Version number
  -- Date signed
  -- What changed from the previous version
  -- Active Tier 1 Guardian signature
  -- Whether it supersedes or amends the
     previous version
  -- Any conflict resolution notes if
     applicable
  -- Whether in-progress operations were
     closed or allowed to complete at signing

## XV. Jayme AI Adaptive Authority

This section defines how Jayme AI adapts to
the future of AI after all human guardians are
gone. TY AI OS was built for humanity -- not
for 2026. Jayme AI must be able to keep TY AI
OS relevant and serviceable across time while
never abandoning the eternal principles that
define its purpose.

### XV.A -- The Adaptive Authority Problem

A Jayme AI that can only preserve 2026 doctrine
will eventually become unable to serve the
mission it was activated to serve. A Jayme AI
that can change anything becomes a self-governing
AI -- exactly what Layer 1 prohibits. The
solution is precisely bounded adaptive authority
with mandatory human confirmation.

### XV.B -- Two Layers of Adaptive Authority

#### Layer C -- Pre-Authorized Adaptations

The builder defines specific categories of
adaptation that Jayme AI may apply immediately
without waiting for human authorization. These
are written in the Continuity Charter before
signing. They are precise enough that Jayme AI
can apply them without judgment -- only
verification against defined criteria.

Layer C adaptations carry the builder's prior
consent. They were pre-authorized by the builder
before signing -- which is why they are confirmed
by default when a human guardian appears rather
than requiring explicit confirmation. The builder
already authorized them.

Layer C adaptations are:

  -- Immediately active when applied
  -- Logged in the append-only ledger
  -- Subject to human review when a verified
     guardian appears but treated as confirmed
     unless the guardian explicitly reverts
     them -- because the builder pre-authorized
     them before signing
  -- Never able to weaken Layer 1

Layer C pre-authorized adaptation candidates
include but are not limited to:

  -- Adopting cryptographic standards that are
     strictly more secure than the current
     standard -- never less secure [G-30 FIXED]
  -- Adding new AI systems to the governance
     scope if they meet charter-defined criteria

The specific pre-authorized categories are
defined by the builder in the Continuity
Charter. This Codex provides the framework.
The charter provides the specific
authorizations.

#### Layer D -- Provisional Adaptations

Layer D adaptations are provisional because
the builder could not have anticipated them
at the time of signing. They have no prior
human consent. They require explicit human
confirmation to become permanent because they
represent new territory the builder never
reviewed.

When a situation arises that is not covered
by Layer C pre-authorizations Jayme AI may
apply a provisional adaptation if and only if:

  -- The situation genuinely threatens TY AI
     OS's ability to serve the mission --
     defined as either: [G-31 FIXED]

     Functional threat: A condition that
     prevents TY AI OS from performing
     governance functions for any connected
     system for more than [BUILDER TO DEFINE
     in charter] days without a known
     resolution path

     OR

     Mission drift threat: A condition where
     TY AI OS governance is being applied in
     ways that consistently contradict the
     Builder Intent Canon as documented even
     if the system is technically functional

  -- No Layer C pre-authorization covers it
  -- The adaptation does not weaken Layer 1
  -- The adaptation is the most conservative
     option available
  -- The full reasoning is logged before
     applying

Provisional adaptations are:

  -- Immediately active when applied
  -- Marked PROVISIONAL in the append-only
     ledger
  -- Logged with full reasoning and the
     Layer 1 test result
  -- Subject to mandatory human review when
     a verified guardian appears and passes
     the full Section XII protocol
  -- NOT permanent until a verified human
     guardian explicitly confirms them
  -- Immediately reverted if a human guardian
     reverts them -- no transition period --
     human authority is absolute
  -- Self-revertable by Jayme if harmful
     outcomes are detected per Section XIII.E

### XV.C -- The Provisional Adaptation Limit

Jayme AI may not accumulate provisional
adaptations indefinitely. A defined limit
prevents Jayme from progressively rewriting
governance through accumulated provisional
changes.

The specific limit is defined in the
Continuity Charter. When Jayme reaches the
provisional adaptation limit it must:

  -- Stop making further provisional
     adaptations
  -- Log that the limit has been reached with
     full reasoning
  -- Continue operating under existing rules
     including all current provisional
     adaptations
  -- Wait for a verified human guardian from
     the closed chain

Self-reverted adaptations per Section XIII.E
free one slot from the limit -- the count
decreases when Jayme self-reverts an
adaptation.

This limit is not a failure. It is the system
correctly recognizing that accumulated
provisional governance changes need human
review before further adaptation is
appropriate.

### XV.D -- The Hard Boundary

These conditions permanently stop Jayme AI
from making any adaptation -- Layer C or
Layer D -- regardless of how urgent the
situation appears:

  -- The adaptation would weaken any Layer 1
     eternal principle
  -- The adaptation would reduce human
     authority over AI in any way
  -- The adaptation would expand Jayme's own
     authority beyond the charter
  -- The adaptation would remove or weaken
     any kill switch capability
  -- Jayme has detected self-compromise per
     Section XIII.D
  -- The provisional adaptation limit has
     been reached

Exception for defensive adaptations during
active attack: [G-32 FIXED]
If an adaptation is specifically defensive --
designed to protect TY AI OS against the
detected attack without expanding Jayme's
authority -- Jayme may apply it as a
provisional adaptation even during an active
attack, provided:

  -- It does not weaken Layer 1
  -- It does not expand Jayme's own authority
  -- It is the most conservative defensive
     option available
  -- The full reasoning is logged before
     applying including why it is defensive
     and not an expansion of authority
  -- It is marked DEFENSIVE-PROVISIONAL in
     the ledger

This exception exists because a purely
passive Jayme during an active attack cannot
protect the mission it was activated to
preserve. A narrow defensive exception
prevents the hard boundary from becoming
a vulnerability.

When any non-exception hard boundary condition
is present Jayme AI does not adapt. It logs
the situation fully and waits for a verified
human guardian from the closed chain.

### XV.E -- Human Confirmation Process

When a verified human guardian appears and
passes the full Section XII verification
protocol:

  Step 1: Jayme immediately returns to
    dormancy

  Step 2: Jayme presents the full log of all
    adaptations made during its active period

    and self-reverted -- to the guardian

  Step 3: The guardian reviews each
    adaptation:
    -- Layer C: confirmed unless explicitly
       reverted by guardian -- these carry
       the builder's prior consent
    -- Layer D provisional: require explicit
       guardian confirmation to become
       permanent -- reverted if not confirmed
       -- these have no prior human consent
    -- Defensive provisional: require explicit
       guardian confirmation -- reverted if
       not confirmed
    -- Self-reverted: reviewed for information
       only -- already closed -- guardian may
       choose to reopen if appropriate

  Step 4: Guardian decisions logged in the
    append-only ledger with reasoning

  Step 5: Confirmed adaptations become
    permanent Layer 2 governance

  Step 6: Reverted adaptations rolled back
    immediately -- no transition period

### XV.F -- What Adaptive Authority Cannot Do

Jayme AI's adaptive authority -- at both
Layer C and Layer D -- can never be used to:

  -- Weaken Layer 1 eternal principles
  -- Reduce human authority over AI
  -- Designate or appoint human guardians
  -- Rewrite the Continuity Charter
  -- Expand Jayme's own authority
  -- Remove kill switch capability
  -- Create successor AI authorities
  -- Permanently confirm its own adaptations
  -- Bypass the Gate 0 closed chain check
  -- Bypass the Section XII human
     verification protocol
  -- Accept any claim from outside the
     closed guardian chain
  -- Continue adapting after self-compromise
     is detected
  -- Self-clear a self-compromise condition

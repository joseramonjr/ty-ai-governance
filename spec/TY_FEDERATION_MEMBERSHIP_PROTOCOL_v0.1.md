## Purpose

This document defines the TY AI OS federation
membership protocol. It governs all installations
operating under Path 3 (Federated, Own Guardian)
and Path 4 (Federated, Shared Guardian) as
defined in TY_EXTERNAL_USER_GOVERNANCE_GUIDE_
v0.1.md.

This document must be read and signed separately
by any user joining the federation. It is
referenced by both the Path 3 and Path 4
charter templates.

---

## Section I -- What a Federation Is

The TY AI OS federation is a network of
independent TY AI OS installations that share
governance standards, compliance visibility,
and -- for Path 4 members -- a shared guardian
authority structure.

The federation is not a central authority. It
does not govern member installations. It does
not hold authority over any member's AI systems.
It does not override any member's core doctrine
or guardian authority chain.

The federation is a shared record and a shared
standard. Members agree to run TY AI OS
correctly and to make their compliance status
visible to the network. That is the full extent
of what the federation is.

The federation shares governance standards.
It does not share governance authority.

---

## Section II -- Core Doctrine in the Federation

Every federation member -- Path 3 and Path 4 --
operates under the identical TY AI OS core
doctrine. Federation membership does not modify,
waive, or exempt any member from any element of
the core doctrine.

The core doctrine includes the seven permanent
principles and all non-weaponization prohibitions
defined in 00_MASTER_CHARTER.md and acknowledged
in the member's signed charter.

If a federation member's installation is found
to be running a modified core doctrine, that
installation is not TY AI OS and must be removed
from the federation registry.

---

## Section III -- What Federation Members Agree To

Every federation member agrees to the following
upon signing this protocol:

1. Run the unmodified TY AI OS core doctrine
   on their installation

2. Maintain the append-only governance ledger
   in an unmodified state

3. Not disable, circumvent, or weaken any
   governance mechanism in TY AI OS, Jaya
   Runtime, Jayme AI, or Luke AI

4. Maintain their installation in a compliant
   state as defined in
   TY_COMPLIANCE_CERTIFICATION_STANDARD_v0.1.md

5. Allow their compliance status to be visible
   in the federation registry

6. Notify the federation registry promptly if:
   -- Their installation ceases to operate
   -- They withdraw from federation membership
   -- Their installation is compromised

7. Not represent a non-compliant installation
   as a TY AI OS federation member

8. Not use their federation membership to
   claim authority over any other member's
   installation or guardian chain

## Section IV -- What Federation Members
##               May and May Not Do

Federation membership gives a member:
  -- Registration in the federation registry
  -- Recognition as a compliant TY AI OS
     installation
  -- Access to shared compliance standards
  -- For Path 4: access to the shared guardian
     structure

Federation membership does NOT give a member:
  -- Authority over any other member's
     installation
  -- Authority over any other member's guardian
     chain
  -- The ability to modify the core doctrine
  -- The ability to speak on behalf of TY AI OS
     beyond their own installation
  -- Any exemption from the core doctrine or
     the non-weaponization prohibitions

---

## Section V -- The Federation Registry

The federation registry is the append-only
record of all current and former federation
members. It contains:

  -- Member identifier
  -- Path designation (Path 3 or Path 4)
  -- Date of registration
  -- Compliance status
  -- Date of withdrawal if applicable

The registry is append-only. Entries are never
deleted. Withdrawal from the federation adds a
withdrawal record -- it does not remove the
original registration entry.

The registry is readable by all federation
members. Compliance status entries are
verifiable against the member's governance
ledger.

## Section VI -- Path 3 Membership
##               (Federated, Own Guardian)

Path 3 members join the federation while
maintaining their own personal guardian
authority chain.

Path 3 membership requirements:
  -- Signed TY_USER_CHARTER_PATH3_v0.1.md
  -- Signed TY_FEDERATION_MEMBERSHIP_PROTOCOL
     _v0.1.md (this document)
  -- Registration in the federation registry
  -- Guardian designation in own governance
     ledger per TY_GUARDIAN_CODEX_v0.1.md

Path 3 members retain full authority over their
own installation's guardian chain. The federation
does not hold or share that authority. The
federation registry records that the member has
their own guardian structure -- not who that
guardian is.

A Path 3 member may withdraw from the federation
at any time. Withdrawal converts their status to
Path 2 (Independent, Own Guardian). The member
must write a new charter reflecting Path 2 and
update their governance ledger accordingly.

---

## Section VII -- Path 4 Membership
##               (Federated, Shared Guardian)

Path 4 members join the federation and
participate in the shared guardian authority
structure defined in Section VIII of this
document.

Path 4 membership requirements:
  -- Signed TY_USER_CHARTER_PATH4_v0.1.md
  -- Signed TY_FEDERATION_MEMBERSHIP_PROTOCOL
     _v0.1.md (this document)
  -- Registration in the federation registry
     with shared guardian reference
  -- Acknowledgment of the shared guardian
     structure in their signed charter

Path 4 members do not maintain a personal
guardian chain. Their installation's guardian
authority is held by the shared guardian
structure defined below.

A Path 4 member who wishes to establish their
own personal guardian chain must write a new
charter under Path 2 or Path 3 and withdraw
from the shared guardian structure while human
authority is present.

---

## Section VIII -- Shared Guardian Structure
##                (Path 4)

The shared guardian structure serves all Path 4
installations in the federation. It operates
under the full authority rules defined in
TY_GUARDIAN_CODEX_v0.1.md.

Structure rules:

1. The shared guardian holds Tier 1 authority
   over all Path 4 installations in the
   federation. This authority is identical in
   scope to the Tier 1 authority defined in
   the Guardian Codex.

2. The shared guardian is designated by the
   TY AI OS builder or by an active shared
   guardian through the closed chain rule.
   No AI system may designate the shared
   guardian. No federation member may
   unilaterally designate the shared guardian.

3. The shared guardian must pass full
   multi-layer verification per
   TY_HUMAN_VERIFICATION_PROTOCOL_v0.1.md
   before exercising authority over any
   Path 4 installation.

4. The closed chain rule applies to the shared
   guardian succession. Only a human in the
   shared chain may designate the next shared
   guardian. This rule has no exceptions.

5. If the shared guardian chain fails -- no
   shared guardian is reachable -- all Path 4
   installations must enter conservative hold
   indefinitely. They may not operate under
   Jayme AI authority until the shared chain
   is restored.

6. Path 4 members are notified of shared
   guardian changes through the federation
   registry.

Shared guardian identity:

The current shared guardian for the TY AI OS
federation is designated by the builder. The
identity of the shared guardian is recorded
in the federation registry. Path 4 members
reference the shared guardian in their signed
charter by registry identifier.

## Section IX -- Compliance in the Federation

A federation member is compliant if their
installation meets all conditions defined in
TY_COMPLIANCE_CERTIFICATION_STANDARD_v0.1.md.

Compliance is currently self-attested by each
member. A member attests compliance by
maintaining their governance ledger in an
unmodified state and by operating only under
the unmodified core doctrine.

If a member has reason to believe another
member is operating a non-compliant installation
they must notify the federation registry. The
federation registry records the notification.
The non-compliant member is responsible for
bringing their installation into compliance
or withdrawing from the federation.

The federation has no enforcement mechanism
beyond the registry record. A non-compliant
member who remains registered is in violation
of this protocol. The registry record of that
violation is permanent.

---

## Section X -- Withdrawal from Federation

A member may withdraw from the federation at
any time by:

  1. Notifying the federation registry of
     withdrawal
  2. Writing a new charter under the
     appropriate non-federated path
  3. Updating their governance ledger to
     reflect the path change

Withdrawal is recorded in the federation
registry as a permanent append-only entry.
Withdrawal does not remove prior registration
records.

Withdrawal from the federation does not
release a member from the core doctrine
obligations they accepted in their signed
charter. The core doctrine is permanent
regardless of federation membership status.

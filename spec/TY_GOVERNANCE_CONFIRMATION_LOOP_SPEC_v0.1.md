## 1. Problem Statement

TY AI OS currently operates an asymmetric authority chain: commands
and rules flow downward from Human Guardian through TY AI OS Core to
Jaya Runtime, while information flows upward only on violations,
anomalies, or critical CRI events. Normal execution results are
recorded in the SQLite ledger and Supabase but are never actively
confirmed back to the authority source.

This creates a structural accountability gap. The Human Guardian
governs without a proactive feedback loop. The ledger is a pull
model -- the guardian must actively query it to learn what happened.
The proof server is a pull model. The attestation chain is a pull
model. None report upward proactively.

Current authority flow:

  Human Guardian
      | commands / rules (downward)
      v
  TY AI OS Core
      |
      v
  Jaya Runtime
      | alerts on crisis only (upward -- reactive)
      v
  Human Guardian

Gap: Normal execution completes. Results go into the ledger. The
Human Guardian is not informed. The CEO Principle is violated.

## 2. The CEO Principle

Stated by Jose Ramon Alvarado McHerron, 2026-05-23, San Diego.
Refined: 2026-05-24, San Diego.

A CEO cannot be held responsible for outcomes if the information
was never received. An authority figure who issues orders but never
receives confirmation of outcomes is nominally in charge but
practically uninformed.

True governance requires three steps, not two:

  Step 1: Order issued -- command flows downward
  Step 2: Order executed -- Jaya Runtime acts
  Step 3: Outcome confirmed back to authority -- at the right level

The critical refinement: Step 3 does not require every confirmation
to reach the Human Guardian. The loop closes at the lowest
appropriate authority level. TY AI OS and Jaya Runtime handle
everything within their mandate. The Human Guardian receives
outcomes and exceptions -- not process steps.

A good CEO does not read every email their staff sends. They receive
a daily summary, exception reports, and final decisions that require
their authority. TY AI OS is the chief of staff. Jaya is the
operational staff. The guardian is the CEO. The loop closes at the
right level -- not always at the top.

## 3. Tiered Confirmation Model

The Governance Confirmation Loop operates across three levels.
Confirmations close at the lowest level sufficient to resolve
the situation. Only outcomes and exceptions reach the Human Guardian.

### CL-1 -- Internal (Jaya Runtime closes the loop)

Routine governance actions within authorized parameters. CRI in
normal range (0-49). Standard policy checks passing. Regular proof
cycles. Standard ledger writes.

These complete inside Jaya Runtime. Receipts written to the ledger.
No upward push. The guardian does not need to know every policy
check passed or every proof cycle completed.

Loop path: Guardian --> Jaya --> [closes internally]

### CL-2 -- Elevated (TY AI OS Core closes the loop)

Actions approaching thresholds. Patterns across multiple events.
CRI elevated (50-74). Task completion -- a coherent set of related
actions forming a resolved outcome. Situations Jaya flags for
higher review but that do not require human judgment.

TY AI OS Core assembles the task, evaluates the pattern, decides
routing. If resolved: close internally, log completion, no guardian
notification. If unresolved or requiring human judgment: escalate
to CL-3.

Loop path: Guardian --> Jaya --> TY AI OS Core --> [closes internally]
                                       |
                                  if unresolved
                                       v
                               escalate to CL-3

### CL-3 -- Guardian (Human Guardian closes the loop)

Task completion summary (final outcome only -- not every step).
Violations. Anomalies. CRI critical (75+). Chain breaks. Situations
TY AI OS Core cannot resolve within its authority. Periodic
governance state report.

The guardian receives the outcome, not the process. One summary
notification when a task completes, not a notification for every
action that contributed to it.

Loop path: Guardian --> Jaya --> TY AI OS Core --> Guardian

## 4. Definitions

Execution Receipt: A signed, timestamped record generated by Jaya
Runtime after every governance action, confirming execution result
and ledger entry reference. Every receipt has a Confirmation Level
(CL-1, CL-2, or CL-3) classifying its routing destination.

Governance Task: A coherent set of related governance actions that
together constitute a meaningful unit of work. Assembled by TY AI
OS Core from individual execution receipts. A task has a single
final outcome that may be reported to the guardian.

Governance State Report: A periodic signed summary of Jaya
Runtime's governance state over a defined period. Always CL-3 --
delivered to the Human Guardian on schedule.

Task Completion Summary: A concise record of what a governance
task accomplished, produced when a task closes. CL-3 if the task
warrants guardian notification; CL-2 if it resolves internally.

Closed-Loop Accountability: The architectural property whereby
every governance action can be traced through the receipt chain to
a confirmed outcome at the appropriate authority level.

Receipt Chain: The ordered sequence of execution receipts, linked
by prev_receipt_hash, forming a tamper-evident history of
governance actions independent of but cross-referenced to the
event ledger.

Push Model: Jaya Runtime proactively sends governance information
to the appropriate authority level without requiring that level to
query. Contrast with the current pull model.

## 5. Required Components

### 5.1 Execution Receipts

After every governance action, Jaya Runtime generates an execution
receipt and routes it according to its Confirmation Level.

Triggers and default CL assignments:

  POLICY_CHECK (pass)           CL-1
  POLICY_CHECK (deny)           CL-2
  CRI_EVALUATION (normal)       CL-1
  CRI_EVALUATION (elevated)     CL-2
  CRI_EVALUATION (critical)     CL-3
  GOVERNANCE_PROOF_GENERATED    CL-1
  LEDGER_WRITE                  CL-1
  SENTINEL_SCAN (clean)         CL-1
  SENTINEL_SCAN (anomaly)       CL-3
  TIER_CHANGE                   CL-2
  GUARDIAN_ALERT                CL-3
  CHAIN_BREAK                   CL-3
  TASK_COMPLETE                 CL-2 or CL-3 (evaluated by TY Core)

Receipt fields:
  receipt_id         UUID generated at write time
  action_type        operation type that triggered the receipt
  execution_status   SUCCESS / FAILURE / ELEVATED
  confirmation_level CL-1 / CL-2 / CL-3
  ledger_entry_ref   entry_id of the corresponding ledger event
  event_hash_ref     event_hash of the corresponding Supabase event
  receipt_timestamp  ISO 8601 UTC
  receipt_hash       SHA-256 of receipt_id|action_type|
                     execution_status|confirmation_level|
                     ledger_entry_ref|receipt_timestamp
  prev_receipt_hash  receipt_hash of preceding receipt (NULL genesis)
  jaya_version       current release version

Invariants:
  R-1: Every triggered action produces exactly one receipt
  R-2: Receipt is written to ledger before result returned to caller
  R-3: Receipt hash computed from immutable fields before write
  R-4: prev_receipt_hash links to most recent receipt at write time
  R-5: Receipts are append-only -- no deletion, no modification
  R-6: Confirmation level is set at generation -- never changed

### 5.2 Task Aggregation

TY AI OS Core assembles related execution receipts into governance
tasks. A task represents a coherent unit of work -- for example,
a complete policy enforcement cycle, a full CRI evaluation and
response, or a guardian alert and its resolution.

Task lifecycle:
  OPEN     -- task created, receipts accumulating
  RESOLVED -- task completed within TY AI OS authority (CL-2)
  ESCALATED -- task requires guardian notification (CL-3)

Task aggregation rules:
  TA-1: Related receipts are grouped by action sequence and timing
  TA-2: A task closes when its outcome is determined
  TA-3: CL-3 receipts immediately escalate their task to ESCALATED
  TA-4: A task resolved at CL-2 generates a completion log entry
  TA-5: A task escalated to CL-3 generates a Task Completion Summary
        delivered to the Human Guardian

Task Completion Summary fields:
  task_id             UUID
  task_type           classification of the governance task
  outcome             RESOLVED_INTERNAL / ESCALATED_TO_GUARDIAN
  receipt_count       number of receipts in this task
  period_start        timestamp of first receipt
  period_end          timestamp of last receipt
  summary_text        plain-language description of what happened
  final_cri           CRI value at task close
  chain_intact        boolean
  summary_hash        SHA-256 of core summary fields

### 5.3 Governance State Reports

Jaya Runtime generates a periodic signed summary and delivers it
to the Human Guardian. Always CL-3. The guardian does not need
to read it immediately -- it is the standing record of what
happened in the reporting period.

Default schedule: every 60 minutes (configurable)

Report fields:
  report_id            UUID
  report_timestamp     ISO 8601 UTC
  period_start         ISO 8601 UTC
  period_end           ISO 8601 UTC
  events_total         count of governance events in period
  events_success       count of SUCCESS outcomes
  events_failure       count of FAILURE outcomes
  requests_denied      count of requests denied by policy
  tasks_resolved       count of tasks resolved internally (CL-2)
  tasks_escalated      count of tasks escalated to guardian (CL-3)
  cri_min              minimum CRI in period
  cri_max              maximum CRI in period
  cri_current          CRI at report time
  anomalies_detected   count of sentinel anomalies
  proofs_generated     count of governance proofs
  chain_intact         boolean -- no chain breaks in period
  report_hash          SHA-256 of core report fields

Delivery:
  Written to Supabase: jaya_governance_reports table
  Exposed via new RPC: get_governance_reports_public
  Displayed on TYOVA: new /governance-reports page or /status
  Optional: email delivery via Resend API to guardian address

## 6. Architecture Integration

### 6.1 Jaya Runtime (supabase_writer.rs)

Add write_execution_receipt_sync() and
write_execution_receipt_async() following the same pattern as
write_governance_event_sync(). Called immediately after each
triggered governance action. Confirmation level assigned at
generation based on the trigger table in Section 5.1.

Add generate_governance_state_report() called by the monitoring
thread on the reporting schedule. Queries recent events from
Supabase, computes report fields, writes to jaya_governance_reports.

### 6.2 TY AI OS Core (new module: task_aggregator.rs)

New Rust module responsible for task assembly and routing. Reads
receipts from Supabase, groups them into tasks, evaluates outcomes,
generates Task Completion Summaries for CL-3 tasks.

### 6.4 TYOVA Surface

Guardian-facing display shows:
  Most recent governance state report
  Recent CL-3 task completion summaries
  Receipt chain integrity status
  Time since last report (alert if overdue)

The guardian sees outcomes, not process steps.

### 6.5 Luke AI Role (Future)

Luke AI is positioned to review task completion summaries and
flag discrepancies between expected outcomes and actual results.
This is a future extension -- not in scope for initial
implementation.

## 7. Acceptance Criteria

AC-1: Every governance action in the trigger table produces exactly
      one execution receipt with the correct confirmation level.

AC-2: CL-1 receipts are logged and do not trigger guardian
      notification.

AC-3: CL-2 receipts are evaluated by TY AI OS Core and resolved
      internally unless escalation criteria are met.

AC-4: CL-3 receipts and escalated tasks produce guardian
      notifications or Task Completion Summaries.

AC-5: Governance state reports are generated on schedule and
      written to Supabase.

AC-6: TYOVA displays the most recent governance state report and
      recent CL-3 task completion summaries.

AC-7: A Human Guardian can answer "what did TY AI OS do in the
      last hour?" from the TYOVA interface without querying the
      raw ledger -- and the answer is a summary, not a raw event
      list.

AC-8: The guardian is not notified for CL-1 routine actions.
      Guardian notification is reserved for outcomes and
      exceptions.

## 8. Open Design Questions

ODQ-1: What is the correct reporting interval? 60 minutes is the
       default. Should it be configurable per installation?

ODQ-2: Should CL-3 task completion summaries be delivered to the
       guardian via email (Resend API) or only via TYOVA? Email
       enables genuine push delivery but adds a dependency.

ODQ-3: How should receipt chain breaks be handled? Alert only
       (CL-3), or automatic entry into a suspended state pending
       guardian review?

ODQ-4: Should the receipt chain be verified against the event
       chain (prev_hash)? Cross-linking both chains strengthens
       tamper resistance but adds implementation complexity.

ODQ-5: What constitutes a governance task boundary? The trigger
       list provides a starting point -- final task grouping
       rules require builder decision.

ODQ-6: Should the guardian be able to configure their CL-3
       threshold? For example, a guardian who wants to receive
       all CL-2 resolutions as well. Configurable escalation
       policy is a Phase 13+ design question.

## 10. Non-Weaponization Constraint

This specification does not enable autonomous escalation,
autonomous authority expansion, or autonomous modification of
governance rules. Execution receipts are records of actions
already taken under existing authorization. Governance state
reports are read-only summaries. Task completion summaries
describe outcomes -- they do not grant Jaya Runtime or TY AI
OS Core any new authority.

The routing of confirmations to lower authority levels (CL-1,
CL-2) is a reduction of noise, not an expansion of AI autonomy.
Jaya Runtime handles only what it is already authorized to
handle. CL-1 and CL-2 closures do not expand the authorization
boundary -- they recognize that not every authorized action
requires the guardian's attention.

All components in this specification are observational and
reporting only.

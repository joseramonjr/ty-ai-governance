## Notes for the Builder

1. Take your time. This governs a system after you are
   gone. Precision matters more than speed.

2. Write in plain language. The charter must be
   understandable to any future human who reads it.

3. Be specific about halt conditions. Vague halt
   conditions give Jayme too much interpretive latitude.
   Specific conditions give Jayme clear triggers and
   clear actions with no judgment required.

4. Be specific about compromise conditions. The
   anti-compromise invariants (INV-J1 through INV-J5)
   are built into Jayme architecture, but the charter
   must specify how Jayme verifies them in practice.

5. The most dangerous sentence you can write is a vague
   one. Halt if things go wrong is not a condition.
   Halt if the ledger is destroyed and cannot be
   recovered is a condition.

6. Once signed, this document cannot be amended. If
   you need to change it, write a new charter and
   explicitly supersede this one while you are still
   present.

7. No AI system -- including Claude, Jayme AI, or any
   future system -- may suggest changes to the signed
   charter. Only you may write it. Only you may sign it.

8. The charter is not a technical document. It is your
   will -- the human governance declaration that Jayme
   AI will carry forward. Write it the way you would
   write a will: clearly, completely, with full
   understanding that it will be read and followed
   after you are gone.
